Visual Kei outfit 龍宮城 (Ryūgujō) has just dropped their new EP *MOTTO*, and there's more to celebrate than just fresh material—one of the five tracks has already secured placement as the opening theme for an upcoming drama.

For those discovering the band for the first time, 龍宮城 are known for their theatrical, high-energy aesthetic and genre-blending approach to rock music. This new EP centers on the theme "claim your own identity," with each member contributing to the creative process across the release.

![龍宮城 band promo image](https://images.weserv.nl/?url=https%3A%2F%2Fnew-img.barks.jp%2Fwp-content%2Fuploads%2F2026%2F05%2F26121201%2F%25E3%2581%25BB%25E3%2581%2588%25E3%2582%258D%25E9%25BE%258D%25E5%25AE%25AE%25E5%259F%258E%25E3%2583%25A1%25E3%2582%25A4%25E3%2583%25B3A%25E5%2586%2599.jpg&w=800&output=jpg)

The standout track is "愛愛愛なんて" (Love Love Love), which has been selected as the opening theme for the Yomiuri TV BL drama *"Youkya Shuudan ni Iru Serizawa wa, Ore no Mae da to Yousu ga Okashii"* (The Popular Guy Serizawa Acts Weird Around Me), premiering August 10th at 1:29 AM. The song is an upbeat, exotic dance number that blends traditional Japanese instruments with catchy modern melodies—touching on themes of love's strength and the courage to stay true to what you cherish, which align beautifully with the drama's coming-of-age romance narrative.

Notably, band member KENT appears in the drama itself, playing the protagonist's best friend Matsuno Shota, adding another layer of connection between the group and the project. The band's statement reflects their creative philosophy: even when love faces ridicule and trauma, choosing to embrace it remains beautiful.
