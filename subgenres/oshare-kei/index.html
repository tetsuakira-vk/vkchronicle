Emerging in the early 2000s, it took the visual spectacle of VK and replaced gothic drama with pop-punk energy, candy colours, and relentlessly upbeat songwriting. Bands wore rainbow-dyed hair, animal prints, and layered street fashion rather than Victorian mourning dress. The fanbase skewed younger; the concerts were celebratory rather than cathartic. At its peak, acts like An Cafe and Lolita23q were pulling enormous crowds on their positivity and accessibility alone. the_sound: | Pop-punk and pop-rock foundations with VK's signature production polish. Songs are short, hook-driven, and built around singalong choruses. Tempos are fast and energetic; guitar tones are cleaner than in heavier subgenres. Synthesisers appear frequently, adding a shimmer to the mix. Vocalists use a higher, cleaner register. Lyrics tend to be about friendship, love, and self-expression rather than darkness or despair. Some acts borrowed from scene and post-hardcore; others leaned into pure J-pop structures. the_look: | An explosion of colour. Hair in every shade — pink, blue, green, often multiple at once. Clothes drawn from Harajuku street fashion: layered items, cartoon prints, platform shoes, excessive accessories. Some acts crossed into decora territory; others kept it to a stylised, candy-coloured version of rock fashion. The unifying principle was that everything should look fun, approachable, and expressive of individuality.
