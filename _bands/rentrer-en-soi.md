Rentrer en Soi emerged as a distinctive force within the Japanese visual kei scene, establishing themselves through their darkly atmospheric and emotionally intense approach to the genre. The band's name, French for "returning into oneself," reflects their introspective artistic philosophy and sets the tone for their sonic identity. Combining the theatrical visual aesthetics characteristic of visual kei with brooding j-rock instrumentation, they crafted a sound marked by melancholic melodies, heavy guitars, and deeply personal lyrical content. Their approach resonated with listeners seeking more psychologically raw and existentially-driven material within the visual kei landscape.

The band became particularly known for their unflinching exploration of dark themes and emotional turmoil, evident in tracks like "I HATE MYSELF AND WANT TO DIE" and "JUST MAD PAIN," which exemplify their uncompromising artistic vision. Rather than relying solely on shock value, Rentrer en Soi grounded their darker aesthetic in genuine musicianship and composition, earning respect within the visual kei community as artists unafraid to venture into uncomfortable emotional territory. Their contribution to the genre helped demonstrate visual kei's capacity for psychological depth and artistic experimentation, influencing the broader landscape of Japanese rock music and cementing their place among the scene's more thought-provoking and boundary-pushing acts.
