Suicide Ali emerged as a distinctive presence within Japan's visual kei movement, bringing a theatrical and avant-garde aesthetic to the genre's landscape. The band carved out their identity through a fusion of visual kei's dramatic visual presentation with intricate j-rock instrumentation, creating a sound that emphasized both technical complexity and emotional intensity. Their approach to the genre reflected the creative experimentation that characterized visual kei during their period of activity, with performances that integrated striking visual elements alongside their musical compositions.

The band's work, including notable tracks such as "行商人の絵画" (The Peddler's Painting) and "四十七の弦" (Forty-Seven Strings), demonstrated their commitment to storytelling through music and image. These compositions showcased their ability to weave intricate arrangements with conceptual depth, establishing them as artists who pushed the boundaries of traditional visual kei sound. Though their tenure in the scene may have been focused, Suicide Ali contributed to the diversity of visual kei's musical and artistic expressions, appealing to listeners who sought sophistication and innovation within the genre's framework.
