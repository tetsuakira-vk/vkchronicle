The GazettE's signature sound—a volatile collision of screamed vocals, distorted guitar riffs, and industrial textures—emerged fully formed from the Kanagawa visual kei underground in 2002, establishing them immediately as something heavier and more aggressive than their peers. Formed by vocalist Ruki, bassist Reita, and lead guitarist Uruha, all veterans of prior visual kei projects, the band completed their classic lineup with guitarists Aoi and drummer Yune. The name itself—an intentional misspelling of "cassette"—signaled their playful defiance of convention from the start.

Their early releases, beginning with the indie single "Wakaremichi" in 2002, sketched the blueprint for a band uninterested in visual kei's more theatrical traditions. Where contemporaries leaned into androgyny and elaborate staging, the GazettE leaned into rage. Their debut album DISORDER arrived in 2004, followed swiftly by NIL in 2006, establishing their capacity for both sonic brutality and compositional sophistication. STACKED RUBBISH, released in 2007, marked a creative apex—a controlled explosion of nu-metal influences, punk energy, and the raw catharsis that would define their approach across decades.

The GazettE's evolution through subsequent releases like DIM, TOXIC, and DIVISION demonstrates a band refusing stagnation. BEAUTIFUL DEFORMITY and DOGMA revealed deepening production ambition and thematic complexity, while albums like NINTH and MASS proved they'd lost none of their destructive power with age. Their consistent catalog speaks to a band that treats each record as both a continuation and a challenge to their previous work—never resting on the visual kei establishment's laurels.

Within the broader landscape of Japanese rock, the GazettE occupy a crucial space as the bridge between visual kei's theatrical heritage and the raw intensity of alternative metal. They've influenced countless bands working in "loud kei" and hardcore territories, proving that visual identity didn't require sacrificing musical aggression. Their 2022 release, a 20th anniversary best compilation titled HETERODOXY—DIVIDED 3 CONCEPTS, underscored their enduring relevance and cultural footprint.

Two decades into their existence, the GazettE remain active and creatively vital—a band whose screams haven't grown hoarse, whose riffs haven't dulled, and whose commitment to challenging both themselves and their audience continues to justify their position as visual kei's most uncompromising architects.
