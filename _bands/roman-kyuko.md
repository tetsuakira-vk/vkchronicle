Roman Kyuko emerged as a distinctive presence in the Japanese Visual Kei scene with a sound that blended the theatrical aesthetics of VK with darker, introspective elements. The band carved out a notable niche through their exploration of melancholic melodies and atmospheric arrangements, positioning themselves among the more experimental acts within the genre. Their visual presentation maintained the elaborate styling characteristic of Visual Kei while their sonic palette incorporated elements that set them apart from more mainstream VK acts, allowing them to develop a dedicated fanbase drawn to their unique artistic vision.

The band's discography, including notable tracks such as "Delete It Mine" and the re-recording of "掃き溜めのメロディ" (Sweeping Floor Melody), showcased their commitment to refining their craft and revisiting their material with evolved perspectives. "Sunset Bitter" further demonstrated their ability to craft emotionally resonant compositions that balanced the genre's dramatic sensibilities with genuine vulnerability. Through their work, Roman Kyuko contributed to the rich diversity of the Visual Kei scene, representing the genre's capacity for artistic experimentation and emotional depth during their active period.
