BORN emerged as a notable act within Japan's visual kei scene, establishing themselves through a distinctive fusion of hard rock aesthetics and theatrical visual presentation. The band is characterized by their dramatic visual style—a hallmark of the visual kei genre—combined with powerful, guitar-driven rock compositions. Their music draws from the broader j-rock tradition while maintaining the genre's signature emphasis on elaborate costumes, makeup, and stagecraft. Songs like "DEMONS" and "RED DESIRE" showcased their ability to craft intense, memorable tracks that resonated with the visual kei fanbase, demonstrating a strong grasp of hooks and dynamic song structures that appealed to both dedicated VK enthusiasts and broader rock audiences.

BORN's significance within the visual kei landscape lies in their commitment to blending visceral musicianship with visual spectacle, contributing to the genre's continued evolution during an era of diverse stylistic experimentation. The band's approach exemplified visual kei's core philosophy of treating music, fashion, and performance art as an integrated whole. Through their energetic live performances and distinctive sonic identity, BORN carved out a respected position among visual kei acts, earning recognition for their dedication to the genre's theatrical traditions while delivering substantial rock credentials. Their work helped sustain visual kei's cultural relevance, appealing to fans who valued both technical musicianship and the genre's characteristic visual excess.
