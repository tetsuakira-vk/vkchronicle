Lynch. stands apart in visual kei's crowded landscape through an uncompromising fusion of crushing metal brutality and soaring melodic sensibility—a band that refuses to choose between heaviness and beauty. Formed in Nagoya in August 2004 by vocalist Hazuki (ex-Deathgaze), guitarist Reo (ex-Gullet), and drummer Asanao (ex-Sicstar), the group officially debuted at Nagoya Club Quattro in December 2004 after initial secret lives under a different name. Guitarist Yusuke (ex-Hybrid-Zombiez) joined the lineup in July to solidify their twin-guitar attack, creating the sonic architecture that would define their entire career.

The band's early albums established their philosophy immediately. *Greedy Dead Souls* (2005) announced their arrival with raw, unpolished fury, while *The Avoided Sun* and *The Buried* (both 2007) refined their approach into a tighter, more devastating package. Throughout the 2010s, albums like *Shadows* (2009), *I Believe in Me* (2011), and the stunning *INFERIORITY COMPLEX* (2012) proved lynch. could evolve without compromise. *Gallows* (2014) marked a creative peak—a concept album that weaponized their contrasts, while *D.A.R.K.* (2015) pushed atmospheric experimentation alongside their signature heaviness. The later *SINNERS -no one can fake my bløod-* (2018) and *Xlll* (2018) demonstrated their refusal to calcify, maintaining relevance through industrial textures and expanded production.

Within Japanese visual kei, lynch. occupies a distinctive position as torchbearers of "loud kei"—the movement prioritizing metallic intensity without surrendering the melodic and theatrical elements that define the genre. Their influence extends beyond the VK community into broader Japanese hardcore and metal circles, proving that visual aesthetics and genuine heaviness aren't mutually exclusive. Over two decades, they've released three EPs, twelve full-length albums, and twenty-five singles, building a catalog of remarkable consistency.

Currently active and still recording, lynch. released *ULTIMA* (2020) and *REBORN* (2023), with *CLIMAX* arriving in 2026. Their recent announcement of reissues and new material confirms they're far from nostalgia acts. Lynch. matters because they've never abandoned their core concept—they've only deepened it, proving that melody and metal aren't opposing forces but partners in catharsis. For Western fans discovering visual kei beyond mainstream acts, lynch. represents the genre's true metallic heart.
