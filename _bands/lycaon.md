Lycaon is a Japanese visual kei band that emerged as a notable act within the dynamic visual kei and J-rock scene. The group is characterized by their striking visual presentation—a hallmark of the visual kei genre—combined with energetic rock compositions that blend heavy instrumentation with melodic sensibilities. Their distinctive aesthetic and sound have garnered them a dedicated fanbase both within Japan and internationally among visual kei enthusiasts, establishing them as contributors to the ongoing evolution of the genre.

Known for tracks like "馬鹿ね。" (Baka ne.) and "Royal Order," Lycaon has demonstrated versatility in their musical approach, crafting songs that showcase both aggressive rock elements and more nuanced arrangements. The band's presence in the visual kei community reflects the genre's continued vitality, with their performances and releases resonating with audiences who appreciate the theatrical presentation and artistic ambition that define visual kei. Through their work, Lycaon has maintained relevance in a scene known for its passionate, devoted fanbase and constant creative innovation.
