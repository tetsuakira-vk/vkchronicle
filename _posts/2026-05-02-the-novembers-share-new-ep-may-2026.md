<p>The Novembers are gearing up for a fresh release this spring: the atmospheric quartet has announced their new EP *合奏する、エンジン* (Ensemble Engine), arriving **May 20, 2026**.</p>
<p>If you haven't encountered The Novembers yet, they're a Japanese rock band known for their moody, introspective soundscapes and layered instrumental arrangements. Their music sits at the intersection of post-rock sensibilities and emotional depth—think cinematic guitar work paired with introspective vocals. They've built a dedicated international following among fans who appreciate nuance and atmosphere in their rock music.</p>
<p>The new EP features four tracks:</p>
<p>1. HERO</p>
<p>2. The Singing Engines</p>
<p>3. 遥か彼方 (Far Away)</p>
<p>4. 合奏 -Hold me Hold me Hold me- (Ensemble)</p>
<p>That tracklist hints at the EP's thematic direction: "The Singing Engines" and the finale's title track suggest themes of collaboration, movement, and interconnection. The poetic Japanese track titles add to the atmospheric quality The Novembers do so well.</p>
<p>This release comes through Virgin Music and is available across digital platforms—you can pre-order or add it to your wishlist via [Virgin Music's store](https://virginmusic.lnk.to/Engines).</p>
<p>For Western fans, The Novembers represent the thoughtful, artistic side of the broader Japanese rock scene—less about shock value, more about emotional resonance and compositional craft. Their EPs and albums reward careful listening, and if you're into bands that prioritize atmosphere and storytelling, this one's worth your attention.</p>
<p>**Mark May 20th on your calendar.** Keep an eye on [their Twitter](https://x.com/THE_NOVEMBERS) for any additional announcements, behind-the-scenes content, or live date news. If you've followed The Novembers before, this is a must-hear. If you're new to them, *合奏する、エンジン* is a perfect entry point.</p>
