<p>Dir En Grey fans rejoice—the legendary visual kei pioneers are heading to Nico Nico Live for an unmissable special broadcast celebrating their latest album, *MORTAL DOWNER*.</p>
<p>For those unfamiliar, Dir En Grey stands as one of visual kei's most influential and artistically ambitious acts. Since their formation in 1997, the Tokyo quintet—fronted by the commanding Kyo alongside guitarists Kaoru and Die, bassist Toshiya, and drummer Shinya—have pushed boundaries with visceral live performances, evolving soundscapes, and conceptually rich releases that blend extreme metal textures with avant-garde sensibilities.</p>
<p>The three-part broadcast campaign kicks off with archive footage. Parts One and Two have already aired, showcasing exclusive performances from their "25th Anniversary Tour 22" and the "PHALARIS Vol. I" setlist (recorded at Osaka's Namba Hatch in July 2023). If you missed them, both videos remain available via timeshift until mid-May—though you'll need to act quickly.</p>
<p>**The main event is Part Three on April 24, 2026, at 9 PM JST.** All five members will appear live to discuss the *MORTAL DOWNER* creative process, the album's dark conceptual world, and their currently running tour. This isn't a scripted Q&A—it's the kind of candid, behind-the-scenes content that rarely makes it to the surface. Better yet, the broadcast is split: the first half streams free to all viewers, while the second half is exclusive to Nico Nico Premium members.</p>
<p>Dir En Grey's willingness to engage directly with fans through unconventional platforms like Nico Nico speaks to their deep connection with their Japanese audience while building momentum for what's clearly a significant chapter in their catalog. *MORTAL DOWNER* represents another evolution in their sound, and hearing the band discuss its genesis firsthand is unmissable.</p>
<p>Mark your calendars and head to the official stream link (https://live.nicovideo.jp/watch/lv350217496) to tune in. Don't miss this rare opportunity to connect with one of visual kei's most important acts.</p>
