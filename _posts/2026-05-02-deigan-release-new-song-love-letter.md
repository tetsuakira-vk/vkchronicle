<p>Rising Visual Kei act 泥眼 (Deigan) has unveiled a striking new single that showcases the band's signature blend of theatrical aesthetics and dynamic musicianship. The track "恋文" (Love Letter) marks another compelling release from the Tokyo-based outfit, and it's well worth your attention.</p>
<p>For those unfamiliar with Deigan, the band has been building steady momentum within the Japanese Visual Kei underground scene. Known for their polished production, intricate guitar work, and front-man 累 (Rui)'s commanding vocal presence, they represent the contemporary wave of VK acts pushing the genre forward with both reverence for tradition and fresh creative vision.</p>
<p>The new song features the full band lineup: vocalist 累 (Rui), guitarist 不律 (Furitsu), support guitarist ALu, bassist 沙夜 (Sayo), and drummer Ayuto. This configuration has proven potent on previous releases, and "恋文" demonstrates why their fanbase continues to grow. The track balances melodic sensibility with the heavier, more aggressive elements that define modern Visual Kei—expect soaring guitar passages, precise rhythmic interplay, and Rui's expressive vocal delivery carrying the emotional weight of the composition.</p>
<p>The title "恋文" translates to "love letter," suggesting romantic or intimate lyrical themes, though as with much of Visual Kei, the theatrical presentation likely elevates the subject matter beyond straightforward sentimentality. The song is available now on YouTube, giving Western fans an easy entry point to experience Deigan's latest work.</p>
<p>If you've been sleeping on this band, now's the perfect time to dive in. Check out the official music video on YouTube, and follow their Twitter (@deigan_official) for updates on future releases and live shows. Visual Kei continues to thrive with acts like Deigan proving that the genre's blend of artistic ambition, technical prowess, and visual spectacle remains as vital as ever.</p>
