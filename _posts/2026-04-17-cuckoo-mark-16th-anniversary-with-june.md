<p>After years away, the theatrical rock outfit **CUCKOO** is making a triumphant return to mark their 16th anniversary. The band has announced a live performance scheduled for **June 28th**, bringing back one of Japan's most intriguing experimental rock projects.</p>
<p>CUCKOO unites three powerhouse musicians from the underground and Visual Kei scenes. Vocalist **Sharaku Kobayashi** (小林写楽) brings his signature dramatic delivery, drawing from his work with METRONOME, FLOPPY, HECTOR, and AINORI TECHNOS. Guitarist **Ao Sakurai** (桜井青) contributes his distinctive sound from cali≠gari and LAB. THE BASEMENT, where he's honed a sharp, angular approach to rock composition. Behind the kit sits **Sasabuchi Hiroshi** (ササブチヒロシ), whose extensive resume includes stints with Plastic Tree, UROBOROS, Velvet Blue, and Tokyo Shoegazer—a pedigree that guarantees technical precision and creative depth.</p>
<p>The combination of these three artists creates something special: CUCKOO sits at the intersection of Visual Kei aesthetics and progressive rock sensibilities, crafting music that's both visceral and intellectually engaging. For those unfamiliar with the project, this anniversary show represents the perfect opportunity to discover (or rediscover) their catalog.</p>
<p>While detailed venue information and ticket details haven't been widely circulated yet, fans should keep an eye on their official Twitter account (@cuckoo20100626) for the latest announcements. Given the caliber of musicians involved and the significance of a 16th-anniversary comeback, this is shaping up to be one of the year's must-see underground live events.</p>
<p>If you've been following any of these members' side projects or have a taste for experimental Japanese rock with theatrical flair, CUCKOO's return deserves your attention. Follow their social media closely for ticket information and setlist updates as the June date approaches.</p>
