<p>Former Girugamesh drummer Ryo Trackmaker is back with fresh material—and it's called "TOXIC."</p>
<p>The solo artist, best known for his work behind the kit in the Japanese metalcore/visual kei crossover outfit **Girugamesh**, has just dropped a brand-new single bearing that provocative title. Released on March 9, 2026, via TuneCore Japan, "TOXIC" marks another step in Ryo's independent journey since his departure from the band.</p>
<p>For those unfamiliar with Ryo's background: Girugamesh was a powerhouse act in the 2000s Japanese underground scene, blending visual kei aesthetics with heavy metalcore riffage and prog influences. The band built a dedicated following for their technical prowess and theatrical presentation before their lineup shifted in the mid-2010s. Since then, Ryo has been carving out his own path as a solo artist and collaborator—proving that the drumming skills and creative vision that made him an asset to Girugamesh are far from diminished.</p>
<p>"TOXIC" comes in at around 3 minutes and arrives as a standalone single release. While we don't yet have details on the track's sonic direction or lyrical themes, the title alone suggests something with edge and attitude—fitting for an artist who spent years at the intersection of heavy music and visual kei theatricality.</p>
<p>For Western VK fans, this is worth paying attention to. Solo ventures from established scene members often showcase a more personal, experimental side of their artistry. Whether Ryo's channeling pure aggression, introspection, or something entirely unexpected, there's every reason to believe "TOXIC" will deliver the technical quality and creative ambition his fanbase has come to expect.</p>
