After stints in Last Cross and Grimm Noir, vocalist Noiru is charting fresh creative territory with a bold new venture: **EL【VAHEM】**.

The project has officially formed, and those curious about the sound should head straight to the demo track. A rough mix of the debut composition is already circulating, giving listeners an early glimpse into what this incarnation will deliver. It's a chance to hear Noiru's artistic direction stripped back to its essentials—raw energy without the polish of a finished production.

For Western fans less familiar with Noiru's history, Last Cross represents significant ground in his career. The band's track "Deneb" showcases the kind of melodic intensity and visual presentation that defined their aesthetic, and it's worth revisiting if you want context for where he's come from. That foundation clearly informs EL【VAHEM】, though this new project signals a willingness to evolve and experiment.

The timing feels significant. Band reformations and new projects have become increasingly common in the Visual Kei scene, but what distinguishes EL【VAHEM】 is the deliberate choice to start from scratch rather than resurrect something familiar. Noiru is inviting fans to grow with him rather than expecting them to simply follow.

The demo's rough-mix status is actually an asset—it removes the barrier between artist and listener, letting you hear the bones of the composition before arrangement and production layers obscure them. Whether you're drawn to Noiru's previous work or discovering him for the first time, this is an accessible entry point.

For the latest announcements, visuals, and updates on EL【VAHEM】, check out the project's official Twitter account. Follow along as Noiru moves forward with what promises to be an intriguing next chapter in his career.
