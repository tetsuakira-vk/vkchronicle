<p>DIV is taking a strategic step back. The reformed Visual Kei act has announced they'll enter a "recharge period" starting November 22, 2026, with the entire year of 2027 dedicated to creative development rather than live performances. Before they disappear into the studio, though, they're embarking on a crucial final tour.</p>
<p>For those unfamiliar with DIV: the band reunited in 2023 after a significant hiatus, with vocalist CHISA and drummer satoshi leading the charge. Their reformed era has been marked by a return to touring and renewed creative energy—but now they're ready to evolve again.</p>
<p>The **PRE-RECHARGE ONEMAN TOUR** kicks off September 25, 2026, spanning nine shows across Japan, from regional cities to the Tokyo finale at Shibuya WWW X on November 22. This tour isn't just a farewell lap—it's positioned as a crucial series that presents where DIV stands now while setting the stage for what comes next. Think of it as the band taking stock before stepping into the unknown.</p>
<p>What's particularly exciting is the visual rebrand accompanying these announcements. New promotional images showcase a distinctly more decadent aesthetic than fans have seen before—satoshi himself redrew artwork inspired by designs from the 2023 reunion shows, signaling a tonal shift. Most intriguingly, **new material is already in development**, designed to complement this fresh visual identity.</p>
<p>DIV is also curating their own festival series, **夏の行方FES 2026** (Summer Direction FES 2026), taking the show across Japan's major cities in May through August. The Tokyo finale on August 20 at Spotify O-EAST features an impressive lineup including BabyKingdom, Chanty, THE MADNA, and more.</p>
<p>The recharge period suggests DIV isn't content resting on reunion laurels—they're plotting something substantial. Whether that means a full-length album, a sonic reinvention, or a visual metamorphosis remains to be seen, but the groundwork they're laying suggests 2027 will be worth the wait.</p>
<p>Grab your **PRE-RECHARGE** tickets and festival passes now. DIV's official site and FC channel (DIV Premium Club on Fanicon) have all booking details. This could be your last chance to catch them before the hiatus.</p>
