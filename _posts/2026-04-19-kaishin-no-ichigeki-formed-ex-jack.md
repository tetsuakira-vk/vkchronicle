<p>Ex-members of Japanese Visual Kei act JACK CAPER have banded together to form **Kaishin no Ichigeki** (かいしんの一撃), and their reveal was nothing short of gaming nostalgia.</p>
<p>The newly formed quartet unveiled themselves on April 19th through a charmingly retro announcement video styled after classic Dragon Quest RPGs—a fitting aesthetic for a band whose name translates to "Critical Hit" or "Fatal Strike." Following the video, each member received individual introductions accompanied by teaser clips of their upcoming sound.</p>
<p>**Who's in the lineup?**</p>
<p>- **Hiro (飛炉)** – Vocals</p>
<p>- **Choco (ちょこ)** – Guitar (also ex-STRESS COMPANY)</p>
<p>- **U** – Bass (also ex-STRESS COMPANY)</p>
<p>- **Suguru (英)** – Drums</p>
<p>While all four members hail from JACK CAPER, they're notably not from the band's final lineup, suggesting this project draws from an earlier era of the group's history. The presence of Choco and U—both of whom also played in STRESS COMPANY—hints at a deeper history of collaboration among these musicians.</p>
<p>**What's Next?**</p>
<p>Kaishin no Ichigeki will make their live debut at **『GAME START』** on **June 22nd at Aoyama RiZM** in Tokyo. The event title cleverly continues their video game–inspired branding and marks the official beginning of their journey as a full band.</p>
<p>For Western Visual Kei fans, this reformation represents an exciting reconnection with musicians from the Japanese underground scene. The gaming-themed rollout and pun-heavy naming suggests a band willing to inject humor and personality into their presentation—a refreshing touch in the scene.</p>
<p>If you caught the original announcement video or have fond memories of JACK CAPER, mark your calendars and keep an eye on their official channels for ticket details and further announcements. This is one debut worth following.</p>
