Plastic Tree is taking fans on a nostalgia trip this summer with a special one-man live celebrating their legendary 1995 debut album *Strange Fruits*.

The Tokyo neo-visual kei pioneers—fronted by vocalist and guitarist Arimura Ryutaro and bassist Hasegawa Masashi—continue their sprawling 30th anniversary campaign with *Plastic Tree Phylogenetic Tree Tour Special Act: A Piece of "Strange Fruits"*. The show hits Ichikawa Bunka Kaikan in Chiba Prefecture on **July 31st, 2026**.

![Plastic Tree 2026 tour announcement](https://images.weserv.nl/?url=https%3A%2F%2Fogre.natalie.mu%2Fmedia%2Fnews%2Fmusic%2F2026%2F0516%2FPlasticTree_tour2026.jpg%3Fimwidth%3D640%26imdensity%3D1&w=800&output=jpg)

Since their 1997 major label debut with "Cracked Window," Plastic Tree have remained one of visual kei's most restless and forward-thinking acts. Their catalog swings wildly—from aggressive guitar-driven assaults to surprisingly melodic pop-leaning cuts—and their theatrical live presence has captivated audiences from Japan to international tours. In 2007, they marked a decade of major-label success with a historic Nippon Budokan performance; they returned to the same venue two years later to prove it was no fluke.

The current *Phylogenetic Tree* campaign, running through 2027, reimagines each chapter of the band's discography as its own live event. This special act focuses on *Strange Fruits*, the debut that set the template for everything to follow. Expect a deep-cut heavy setlist diving into one of visual kei's most formative records.

Official fan club members can secure tickets through the first-wave lottery drawing, which runs through **May 19th, 2026**. This is a must-see for anyone invested in the band's legacy or curious about neo-visual kei's foundational era.

For ticket details and full tour information, head to Plastic Tree's official channels and streaming platforms where you can revisit *Strange Fruits* ahead of the show.
