When a legendary Japanese rock band decides to throw a three-day arena extravaganza culminating in their bassist's birthday celebration, you know it's going to be unforgettable. 黒夢 (Kuroyume) just pulled off exactly that at Tokyo's TOYOTA ARENA TOKYO from July 17–19, with the finale on the 19th doubling as a jubilant tribute to bassist Hitoji's 54th birthday.

For those unfamiliar, 黒夢 are veterans of Japan's rock scene, fronted by the charismatic Kiyoharu on vocals. The band has built their reputation on raw, theatrical rock that balances aggression with genuine emotion—a formula that clearly still resonates with packed arena crowds nearly three decades into their existence.

Day three kicked off early at 4:07 PM (unusually early for a Japanese rock show) with a cover of Steppenwolf's "Born to Be Wild" blaring over the PA. Hitoji had selected the entire setlist for this finale, and it showed remarkable diversity. The band tore through "Spray," "Drive," and "Can't See Yard" before Hitoji addressed the crowd, encouraging fans to soak in "this scenery" as a treasured memory.

![黒夢 live performance](https://images.weserv.nl/?url=https%3A%2F%2Fnew-img.barks.jp%2Fwp-content%2Fuploads%2F2026%2F07%2F21054544%2F01_260719_mori_0592-1024x682.jpg&w=800&output=jpg)

The middle section delivered classics like "BARTER," "MASTURBATING SMILE," and selections from the *CORKSCREW* album, building toward a stunning closer: "FAKE STAR"—typically an opener—played as the show's final full-band track. Kiyoharu's declaration "We are ROCK 'N' ROLL" electrified the room.

But the real magic came in the encore: Guitarist Daiki launched into "Happy Birthday" while a massive cake was wheeled onstage. The touching moment saw the entire venue join in celebrating Hitoji, with Kiyoharu reflecting, "What a future this is—two years in a row celebrating Hitoji's birthday together."

![Hitoji birthday celebration](https://images.weserv.nl/?url=https%3A%2F%2Fnew-img.barks.jp%2Fwp-content%2Fuploads%2F2026%2F07%2F21055827%2F01_260719_mori_0137-1024x682.jpg&w=800&output=jpg)

The set closed with emotional anchors "Maria," "少年" (Shounen), and "BEAMS"—the latter featuring Kiyoharu embracing Hitoji mid-performance while singing about clinging to silver love forever.
