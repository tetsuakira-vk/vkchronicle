After six years of creative collaboration, visual kei outfit GREN has announced they will disband, with their final performance taking place on September 30, 2025, at Shibuya Rex in Tokyo.

The news came via official statement from the band, marking the end of a chapter that began when vocalist ߺ-Rey- (formerly of ASTARIA) joined the group in 2019. GREN formed around a core vision of blending theatrical aesthetics with accessible, emotionally resonant songwriting—a formula that resonated with Western fans discovering the band through Japan's thriving underground scene.

The decision to disband comes after considerable deliberation within the band. According to their statement, the members have reached a natural conclusion to their creative journey and felt it was the right time to move forward. Rather than fade quietly, GREN has chosen to celebrate their legacy with a final hometown show that longtime supporters won't want to miss.

GREN's discography includes notable releases like *Aster* (2019), *pillow talk* (2020), *INSIDE GREN* (2024), and *連鎖 -Ren Sa-* (2024). The band's lineup featured ߺ-Rey- on vocals, RUNA on guitar, and a rotating rhythm section that ultimately settled on support members. Their sound—a blend of melancholic melodies and driving instrumentation—carved out a distinctive space in the modern visual kei landscape.

The Shibuya Rex farewell show represents a chance for fans, both longtime devotees and curious newcomers, to witness the band's final performance. For those unable to attend in person, GREN's recorded catalog remains an excellent entry point into their artistry.

If you've followed GREN's journey or are discovering them now, mark your calendars for September 30. Even though the band's chapter is closing, their music will endure.
