D'ESPAIRSRAY are treating their dedicated fanbase to an intimate, members-only experience this September — and it's already shaping up to be one of the year's most exclusive Visual Kei events.

The legendary Tokyo act has announced a special fan club live at Liquid Room on September 9, 2026, exclusively for members of their official fan club, [un]Beautiful. This isn't just a standard concert: the setlist will be partially determined by fan voting, making this a truly collaborative event between band and supporters.

For those unfamiliar with D'ESPAIRSRAY, they're one of Japan's most influential Visual Kei acts, known for their dark, atmospheric sound and theatrical presentation. After a 15-year hiatus, they made a triumphant return and are now entering what the band describes as a new creative phase. This fan club live perfectly encapsulates their renewed focus on creating intimate spaces with their passionate community.

The September show combines a full live performance with a "basement meeting corner" — an intriguing concept that suggests exclusive interactions beyond the typical concert experience. Every attendee receives an exclusive [un]Beautiful sticker as an in-venue bonus. Tickets are priced at ¥9,900 and will be available through a tiered system beginning May 28, 2026, with international fan club members eligible to participate alongside Japanese fans.

The song voting campaign runs through July 28, giving supporters nearly two months to influence the setlist. It's the kind of hands-on fan engagement that builds genuine community and demonstrates D'ESPAIRSRAY's commitment to their supporters.

Beyond this September date, D'ESPAIRSRAY also have a major New Year's Eve live scheduled at KT Zepp Yokohama on December 30, 2026 — part of their broader "CLIMAX" tour.

Interested in joining [un]Beautiful or securing tickets? Head to D'ESPAIRSRAY's official site at despairsray.com for membership details and ticket information. Their latest single "Rapture" is available on YouTube to get you hyped in the meantime.
