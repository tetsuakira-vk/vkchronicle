In a tongue-in-cheek official announcement that had fans doing a double-take, Japanese Visual Kei outfit Deshabillz have "fired" guitarist Erina—though the circumstances are far more amusing than they are serious.

According to the band's statement, Erina was terminated from her position for "being distracted by the bartender and making mistake after mistake." The playful tone and specificity of the accusation make it clear this is the kind of in-house ribbing that tight-knit bands are known for, likely poking fun at a real or exaggerated incident during rehearsals or a live performance.

For those unfamiliar with Deshabillz, the band represents the contemporary Visual Kei scene's commitment to theatrical presentation and dark aesthetics. Like many acts in the genre, they balance serious musicianship with a sense of humor and spectacle that keeps their fanbase engaged both on and off stage.

This kind of announcement—part roast, part kayfabe—is practically a time-honored tradition in Visual Kei. Bands often use their official channels to share inside jokes with their community, strengthening the bond between members and listeners. It's the sort of content that spreads quickly among Western fans who appreciate the genre's willingness to not take itself too seriously, even when delivering heavy, intricate music.

Of course, Erina wasn't actually going anywhere. The "firing" serves as a humorous public callout that likely resulted in good-natured ribbing from bandmates and fans alike. These moments remind us why Visual Kei maintains such a devoted international following—the artists understand that connecting with fans extends beyond the music itself.

If you want to stay in the loop on Deshabillz's latest updates, performances, and inevitable future band drama, check out their official channels and social media presence. They're well worth following if you're into the darker, more theatrical corners of the Visual Kei universe.
