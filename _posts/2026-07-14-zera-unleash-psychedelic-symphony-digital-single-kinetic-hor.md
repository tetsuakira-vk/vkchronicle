Zera are ramping up the intensity this fall with a brand-new digital single and an ambitious nationwide tour that promises to be anything but conventional. The Tokyo-based outfit—known for their genre-bending approach to visual kei—are dropping "Psychedelic Symphony" on September 9th, and they're backing it with a sprawling one-man tour called the "Kinetic Horror Show."

For those new to Zera, the band has carved out a distinctive niche by fusing psychedelic textures with aggressive musical frameworks, refusing to be boxed into a single aesthetic. Vocalist 氷翠 (Hyoumi), guitarist KIRAN, second guitarist ミナギ (Minagi), and bassist 流 (Ryu) create soundscapes that are simultaneously cerebral and visceral—a balance that keeps Western fans increasingly interested in what they're doing.

![Zera band visual](https://images.weserv.nl/?url=http%3A%2F%2Fvijuttoke.com%2Fcontents%2Fwp-content%2Fuploads%2Fph%25E3%2582%25BC%25E3%2583%25A9%25E3%2583%2584%25E3%2582%25A2%25E3%2583%25BC-580x820.jpg&w=800&output=jpg)

The new single itself is described as both inventive and confrontational—exactly what you'd expect from a band that refuses easy categorization. A full music video accompanies the track, giving fans both sonic and visual material to sink their teeth into.

The "Kinetic Horror Show" tour spans the entire nation, kicking off September 12th in Osaka and wrapping December 7th at Shinjuku Loft. Twelve dates in total, hitting major cities including Nagoya, Ikebukuro, Okayama, Takamatsu, Kobe, Fukuoka, and more. Pre-sale tickets generally run ¥4,500 (advance) or ¥5,000 (door), with the final show pricing at ¥5,000 advance and ¥5,500 at the door—all before drink charges.

Beyond the main tour, Zera are keeping busy with additional appearances through the summer and early autumn, including festival slots and collaborative shows.
