<p>Dir En Grey's collaboration with Tower Records Cafe is making a triumphant return—and Western fans should take note of how Japanese record stores celebrate their biggest acts.</p>
<p>Due to popular demand, the DIR EN GREY × TOWER RECORDS CAFE takeout bottle collaboration is being revived at two locations this May. Originally launched to commemorate the band's 12th studio album *Mortal Downer*, these limited-edition bottles sold out so quickly that Tower Records decided to bring them back for an encore run.</p>
<p>For those unfamiliar with Dir En Grey, they're one of Japan's most influential heavy rock exports—a band that's built a global fanbase through relentless touring, inventive songwriting, and an unmistakable visual identity that blends brutalism with theatrical flair. Their new album *Mortal Downer* showcases the band's evolution while maintaining the sonic intensity their devoted international audience expects.</p>
<p>**Here's what you need to know:**</p>
<p>The collaboration features takeout bottles filled with caramel popcorn (¥980 / ~$6.50 USD) in custom packaging designed with Dir En Grey's collaboration artwork. Each bottle purchase comes with a random bonus trading card. Limit: 6 bottles per person.</p>
<p>**Dates and Locations:**</p>
<p>**Umeda NU Chayamachi** (Osaka): May 8–11, 2026 | 1:20 PM–8:50 PM  </p>
<p>6F Takeout Counter, NU Chayamachi, Kita-ku, Osaka  </p>
<p>[Store X](https://x.com/TRC_NUchaya) | [Store Website](https://tower.jp/restaurants/cafe/umedanuchayamachi)</p>
<p>**Nagoya Sakae Skyle** (Aichi): May 22–25, 2026 | 1:00 PM–8:30 PM  </p>
<p>9F Takeout Counter, SKYLE, Naka-ku, Nagoya  </p>
<p>[Store X](https://x.com/TRC_nagoya) | [Store Website](https://tower.jp/restaurants/cafe/nagoyasakaeskyle)</p>
<p>No advance reservation needed—first-come, first-served. However, if demand is high, the cafes will distribute numbered tickets to manage crowds. Stock is limited and replenished intermittently, so it's worth checking each location's Twitter account for availability updates.</p>
<p>**Important:** This is takeout-only. You cannot consume the bottles inside the cafe or Tower Records building. Also note the aluminum lid has sharp edges, so be careful when opening.</p>
<p>If you're in Japan during these dates or have friends visiting, this is a rare chance to own official Dir En Grey merchandise while supporting the band through Tower Records' beloved cafe collaboration series.</p>
