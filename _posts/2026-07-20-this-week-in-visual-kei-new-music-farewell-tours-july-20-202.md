What a week for the Visual Kei scene. We've got fresh singles dropping left and right, bands hitting the road in force, and some genuinely bittersweet news mixed in with the celebration. Buckle up—there's a lot to unpack.

Let's start with the music. Damian Hamada's Creatures came through with "Seven Deadly Sins," a heavy metal banger that's already getting rotations across Western VK circles. Meanwhile, Zera leaned into their psychedelic side with "Psychedelic Symphony," a digital single paired with what promises to be a genuinely unhinged tour. Sick² and Bimi also dropped singles this week—"Mouth to Mouth" and "Voice" respectively—so your playlist is definitely getting upgraded. Golden Bomber's Utahiroba even landed a BizCrysta TV commercial spot, which... honestly, we love to see it.

The live circuit is absolutely popping off. Zilqy declared a "Birth of Riot" following a triumphant one-man show, which feels like a moment worth celebrating. More importantly, Verde/ and ASAGI just shared a stage for a historic 2-man live that's already being talked about as a genuine event. Rekishi announced an acoustic tour for 2026 with new visuals, and Sick² is launching a nationwide tour to support their new single. Seek's Psycho le Cému universe got a boost too, welcoming GOTCHAROCKA to their Fish Dance Hour Nico Nico broadcast—a crossover that feels genuinely exciting.

On the heavier side: Kizu called it quits after nine years. That's the kind of news that stings a little, but their legacy in the scene feels solid. Fukuro's announcement of an indefinite hiatus amid allegations is messier and genuinely disappointing, especially given the band's profile. And Kasumisouto Stella's Kawana Hikaru pivoted toward idol territory with a debut photo book release, which might signal some interesting new directions for where some VK musicians are heading.

Amai Bouryoku's band profile piece reminded us that the scene's got layers of history we sometimes overlook—eight years deep and still going.

It's been a week of contrasts, honestly: simultaneous highs and lows, new tours and farewell announcements, studio releases and live events stacked on top of each other. The Visual Kei machine keeps churning, and that's what we're here for.

Watch next week for tour date confirmations and whether any of these new singles crack the Japanese rock charts.
