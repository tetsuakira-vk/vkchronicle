<p>Dir En Grey are celebrating the release of their 12th studio album *Mortal Downer* in style—and Western fans should take note of what's happening in Japan right now, because limited-edition collaboration merch is flying off shelves.</p>
<p>The legendary Japanese heavy rock band has partnered with Tower Records Cafe for an exclusive takeout collaboration running through late April 2026 at three locations: Umeda (Osaka), Nagoya, and Fukuoka. If you've ever wondered why Dir En Grey command such devoted global support, this kind of fan-centric rollout is part of the answer—they treat each album release like an event.</p>
<p>**What's on offer?**</p>
<p>The centerpiece is a **Dir En Grey original label beer** (¥1,100 / ~$7.50 USD) featuring the *Mortal Downer* album artwork. Each purchase comes with a random bonus trading card. It's limited to six per person and only available at the Umeda and Fukuoka locations—the Nagoya store is bottle drinks only.</p>
<p>There's also a **Black Popcorn takeout bottle** (¥980 / ~$6.70 USD) designed with exclusive collaboration artwork. Same deal: six per person, one bonus card per purchase.</p>
<p>**The timeline:**</p>
<p>- **Umeda NU Chayamachi (Osaka):** April 7–26, 12:30–20:00</p>
<p>- **Nagoya Sakae Skyle:** April 10–26, 12:30–20:00</p>
<p>- **Fukuoka:** April 10–26, 12:30–20:00</p>
<p>Items are split into early and late-period releases with different card designs—a classic Japanese strategy to encourage multiple visits. No reservations needed; it's first-come, first-served, though they'll hand out numbered tickets during busy periods.</p>
<p>Dir En Grey continue to prove why they're one of Japan's most enduring heavy acts. *Mortal Downer* marks a significant milestone, and this cafe collab captures the band's ability to blur the line between underground credibility and mainstream appeal. For Western fans planning Japan trips in April, these locations are worth the pilgrimage.</p>
<p>Check the official Tower Records Cafe social media accounts (linked in each store's details) for real-time stock updates—these limited items won't last long.</p>
