<p>A fresh face is joining the Visual Kei underground: **EPHEM** (エフェメ) has officially formed and already locked in their debut live performance for **April 6, 2026**.</p>
<p>What makes EPHEM particularly interesting is the lineup's pedigree. Vocalist **Emiru** brings extensive experience from multiple projects including T@Planet, Clown×Crown, Altair, and Rabyrinth—a track record that signals serious ambition for the new venture. The guitar support slot features **Utaha**, another T@Planet and Clown×Crown veteran, suggesting these musicians have a long-standing creative chemistry worth watching.</p>
<p>The full roster includes:</p>
<p>- **Vocal:** Emiru</p>
<p>- **Guitar:** Nazora</p>
<p>- **Guitar Support:** Utaha</p>
<p>- **Bass:** Shiu (ex-Sarcastic, KANASHIVARI, HUMAN ERROR)</p>
<p>- **Drums Support:** Niy (Luvielle, THE MAYDAY MAYDAY)</p>
<p>Shiu's background in bands like Sarcastic and KANASHIVARI indicates EPHEM may lean toward heavier, more aggressive Visual Kei territory—though we'll need to hear their actual sound before drawing conclusions.</p>
<p>The band's name itself carries symbolic weight. "Ephemeral" speaks to transience and beauty in the moment, a fitting choice for a Visual Kei project that embraces the genre's theatrical, sometimes fleeting nature.</p>
<p>With their debut live just over a year away, EPHEM has time to build anticipation and develop their visual identity—arguably as crucial as the music itself in Visual Kei. The involvement of established underground figures suggests this isn't a casual side project, but a deliberate creative statement.</p>
<p>Western fans should keep tabs on their Twitter (@ephem_official) for updates on their sound, aesthetic direction, and whether additional live dates get announced before April 2026. If the members' previous work is any indication, EPHEM could become a notable addition to the current VK landscape.</p>
