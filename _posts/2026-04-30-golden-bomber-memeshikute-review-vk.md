<p>When Golden Bomber unleashed *Memeshikute* in 2009, they captured something that had been simmering beneath the surface of their theatrical rock aesthetic: pure, unfiltered emotional devastation wrapped in glitter and defiance. This CD+DVD maxi single isn't just a release—it's a statement of artistic maturity from a band that refused to be confined by Visual Kei's genre conventions.</p>
<p>The title track "Memeshikute" is an absolute masterclass in controlled chaos. Built on a foundation of driving percussion and layered synths, the song showcases frontman Keiji-san's vocals at their most vulnerable yet commanding. There's a palpable tension throughout: the production balances delicate melodic passages with sudden explosions of distorted guitars, creating an emotional rollercoaster that feels earned rather than exploitative. The song's progression refuses to follow predictable patterns, which is precisely why it works so effectively as the album's centerpiece.</p>
<p>What makes *Memeshikute* essential listening is how it positioned Golden Bomber within the broader 2000s Visual Kei landscape. While bands around them were either leaning into electronica or retreating into nostalgia, Golden Bomber synthesized their theatrical influences into something distinctly contemporary—catchy enough for mainstream appeal, but with enough compositional complexity to satisfy die-hard VK purists. The production quality here is pristine without sounding sterile; you can hear every instrument's contribution without anything disappearing into a wall of sound.</p>
<p>The DVD component featuring the "Memeshikute" music video adds crucial visual context. Golden Bomber's presentation has always been integral to their identity, and this era captures them at a particularly striking moment—costumes ambitious but not overwrought, aesthetic choices purposeful rather than random. The video reinforces what the audio already communicates: this is a band confident in their vision.</p>
<p>In the broader context of Golden Bomber's discography, *Memeshikute* represents a sweet spot where their experimental tendencies and their desire for accessibility found perfect equilibrium. It's neither their most accessible work nor their most daring, but it's arguably their most *complete*.</p>
<p>The only minor criticism is that as a maxi single, the package feels slightly lean—even with the DVD, you're left wanting more material to sink your teeth into. But what's here is genuinely excellent.</p>
<p>**Recommendation:** Essential for Visual Kei fans seeking proof that the genre could evolve beyond its established templates in the late 2000s. Highly recommended for anyone exploring Golden Bomber's catalog.</p>
