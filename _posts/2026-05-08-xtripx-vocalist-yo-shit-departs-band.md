xTRiPx has announced the departure of vocalist Yo-shiT, marking a significant shift for the Tokyo-based Visual Kei outfit. The announcement came on August 20, 2025, through the band's official channels and the Japanese Visual Kei database vkdb.

For those unfamiliar with xTRiPx, the band has been a fixture in the underground and mid-tier Visual Kei scene since the early 2010s, known for their energetic performances and evolving sound across releases like *Astrovibes* (2010), *Disturb[da]bordeR* (2011), and *Loud[&]round* (2014). Yo-shiT's tenure as frontman spanned from the band's formation through numerous lineup changes and tours, making his departure emotionally significant for the fanbase.

While the official announcement does not detail the reasons behind his exit, departures of this scale often precede either a band hiatus, reformation with a new vocalist, or dissolution. xTRiPx's social media presence remains active, suggesting the band is evaluating its next steps rather than disbanding entirely.

The band's history reflects the resilience typical of mid-tier Visual Kei acts. Beyond Yo-shiT, xTRiPx cycled through multiple bassists over the years—including Ruchi (who departed in 2008), unnamed members (2010), and most recently Naoto (who joined in 2015). Drummer ena remains a constant presence, while guitarist K has been a core member throughout the band's existence.

For long-time supporters, this news represents the end of an era. Yo-shiT's vocal delivery—a blend of melodic clarity and emotional intensity—was central to tracks across xTRiPx's catalog. The band's last documented activity included performances at major festivals and underground venues across Japan.

**What happens next?** Visual Kei fans should keep tabs on xTRiPx's social media channels and vkdb for announcements regarding a potential replacement vocalist or any clarification on the band's future. If you've followed xTRiPx's journey, now is the time to revisit their discography and show support however you can—concert tickets, merchandise, or simply streaming their back catalog.
