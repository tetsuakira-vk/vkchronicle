<p>If you've ever wondered what goes on behind the velvet curtain of visual kei's biggest productions, MASKED just gave you an all-access pass.</p>
<p>The collective has released a two-part documentary capturing the making of their ambitious MASKED 2026 photoshoot—a massive undertaking that brought together 47 vocalists from across the contemporary visual kei scene. These aren't just any artists; they represent the current wave of talent pushing the movement forward, from established names to rising acts defining the sound and aesthetics of modern VK.</p>
<p>The documentary is split across two episodes (DAY1 and DAY2), offering raw footage from the photoshoots themselves, candid interviews with participating artists, and their reflections on the state of visual kei as a whole. It's the kind of high-production-value content that's genuinely rare in the VK community—most behind-the-scenes material tends to be rough bootlegs or brief clips. This is slick, well-edited, and genuinely insightful for fans who want to understand how these massive collaborative events come together.</p>
<p>MASKED has built a reputation over the years as tastemakers and curators of the scene. Rather than promoting a single band, they celebrate visual kei as a unified movement, and this documentary is a love letter to that ethos. Whether you're curious about the creative process, want to discover new vocalists, or simply love seeing your favorite artists interact, these videos are worth your time.</p>
<p>The actual MASKED 2026 event will take place **May 22–23** across three Tokyo venues: Spotify O-EAST, Spotify O-WEST, and duo MUSIC EXCHANGE. Tickets are already on sale, and the organizers have set up special links for international fans who want to participate in this celebration of contemporary visual kei.</p>
<p>If you watch the documentary, make sure to like and comment—supporting high-quality VK content like this signals to creators that Western fans are invested and engaged. Head to the official MASKED website to secure your tickets now.</p>
