muque have unveiled the music video for "HAPPY GROOVY," a standout track from their second full-length album released in April that's already become a live favorite among their fanbase. The band—known for their distinctive blend of hybrid, synth-driven pop-rock aesthetics and frontman Asakura's characteristically laid-back vocal delivery—crafted something genuinely introspective with this one.

The song explores a deeply relatable emotional journey: learning to accept yourself despite constantly worrying about others' perceptions, and ultimately embracing your own perceived weaknesses as part of who you are. Rather than spelling this out bluntly, the MV visualizes this transformation through a clever spatial metaphor—moving from a claustrophobic office environment into an open, liberating outdoor setting that feels almost dreamlike in its execution.

![muque performing on stage](https://images.weserv.nl/?url=https%3A%2F%2Fnew-img.barks.jp%2Fwp-content%2Fuploads%2F2026%2F03%2F02185607%2Fgroup_main_1047_0131.jpg&w=800&output=jpg)

What makes this video worth repeated viewing is how deliberately it scatters symbolic imagery throughout—little visual breadcrumbs that reward closer attention. Even if you've already worn out the audio version, the accompanying visuals add genuine depth to the song's thematic weight, opening up new interpretations each time you watch.

For those who've already connected with muque's sound, this is essential viewing. And if you're new to them, "HAPPY GROOVY" is an excellent entry point into what makes the band tick.

**2026 Tour Dates:**

muque are hitting the road across Japan in spring 2026 as part of their "GLHF" live tour. Dates span from March 3 (Kanazawa EIGHT HALL) through April 19 (Tokyo Zepp Haneda), with stops in Nagano, Sendai, Morioka, Kumamoto, Nagasaki, Okayama, Utsunomiya, Mito, Sapporo, Osaka, and Nagoya. Venue capacities range from smaller clubs to major Zepp locations, so check official channels for ticketing and the full details on doors and start times.
