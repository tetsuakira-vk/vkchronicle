<p>Strawberry Song Orchestra (ストロベリーソングオーケストラ) have made their mark with the release of their latest single "屋根裏ノメロディ" (Attic Melody) on April 4th, and the band is stepping into a bold new era with a refreshed visual direction.</p>
<p>The Tokyo-based outfit has built a reputation for their theatrical approach to Visual Kei, blending orchestral arrangements with gothic sensibilities and meticulously crafted aesthetics. With Attic Melody, they've delivered another compelling addition to their catalogue, paired with an official music video that showcases the band's evolving image.</p>
<p>The accompanying MV—available now on their official YouTube channel—demonstrates the cinematic ambition SSO consistently brings to their visual output. The track itself reflects the band's signature sound: lush instrumental layers, dramatic vocal delivery, and the kind of atmospheric depth that rewards repeated listens. The title's imagery of "attic melodies" evokes nostalgia and hidden emotional spaces, thematic territory that fits squarely within the band's preferred lyrical landscape.</p>
<p>What's particularly striking about this release is the band's new look, shared across their official social media channels. The updated aesthetic signals a creative evolution while maintaining the theatrical DNA that defines their approach to Visual Kei. Whether this marks a broader stylistic shift or simply a seasonal refresh, the visual refresh suggests Strawberry Song Orchestra are committed to keeping their presentation as compelling as their music.</p>
<p>For Western fans still navigating the depths of Japan's Visual Kei scene, SSO represent the kind of mid-tier act that deserves wider recognition—polished, theatrical, and genuinely innovative without chasing trends. If you haven't encountered them yet, Attic Melody is an excellent entry point.</p>
<p>Head over to their official YouTube channel to check out the MV, and follow their Twitter (@15sso_official) for future announcements. SSO are definitely a band worth keeping on your radar.</p>
