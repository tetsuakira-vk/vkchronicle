<p>A supergroup of seasoned Visual Kei veterans is dropping their third EP this summer, and it's shaping up to be essential listening for fans of Japan's underground rock scene.</p>
<p>**the superlative degree** will release their new digital EP "コモンセンス" (Common Sense) on **June 15, 2026**. The project brings together an impressive lineup of musicians with deep roots in the VK underground: vocalist Akito Hashizume (formerly of ALL I NEED and HUSH), guitarists YUJI (ex-SECRET, Galla) and KENJI (an extensively experienced player from LAID, CLOSE, as.milk, ARBEIT, and THE TERROR'S 666), bassist Hiroyuki Takase (ex-[dart], HUSH), and drummer SHINGO (ex-JURASSIC, THE TERROR'S 666).</p>
<p>If those credits look familiar, there's good reason—this lineup represents a cross-section of Japan's mid-tier Visual Kei and alternative rock world, bringing together musicians who've spent years honing their craft in the Tokyo underground. The combination of these individual trajectories suggests a band with serious musical maturity and artistic vision beyond the typical supergroup novelty.</p>
<p>With two EPs already under their belt, the superlative degree has been steadily building momentum. "コモンセンス" marks their third digital release, and given the caliber of musicians involved, expectations should be high. The title itself—"Common Sense"—hints at a band willing to challenge conventions, which is fitting for a project assembled from members of bands spanning different eras and aesthetics within the Visual Kei sphere.</p>
<p>The digital-only format means this will be easily accessible for Western fans without the usual import hassles, making it an ideal entry point if you're curious about what these veterans are cooking up together.</p>
<p>Keep your eyes on the superlative degree's **[Twitter account](https://x.com/the_s_degree)** for more details closer to the release date. Mark June 15, 2026 on your calendar—this one's worth your attention.</p>
