<p>DIR EN GREY continue to push their theatrical darkness with a haunting new music video that captures the oppressive atmosphere of their latest album.</p>
<p>On April 24, the legendary Visual Kei pioneers unveiled the "promotional edit" of **MOBS**, a track from their recently released album **MORTAL DOWNER**. The video clocks in under four minutes—a condensed take on the album version, which stretches past the six-minute mark—making it perfectly sized for immediate visual impact without overstaying its welcome.</p>
<p>For those unfamiliar with DIR EN GREY: the band has been a cornerstone of Visual Kei since the late 1990s, evolving from their earlier gothic roots into a more avant-garde, industrial sound that refuses easy categorization. Led by vocalist Kyo, the group has consistently challenged both the aesthetics and sonic boundaries of the genre, blending harsh instrumentation with deeply theatrical visuals.</p>
<p>**MORTAL DOWNER**, which dropped earlier in April 2026, represents another chapter in the band's uncompromising artistic vision. The **MOBS** video aesthetic aligns perfectly with the album's thematic darkness—you can expect the brooding, intense visual language that DIR EN GREY fans have come to expect. The decision to release a shorter "promotional" cut suggests the full video experience may offer even more layered detail for patient viewers.</p>
<p>The teaser campaign leading up to the video release had already generated considerable buzz within the Visual Kei community, and the finished product doesn't disappoint those hungry for new DIR EN GREY material.</p>
<p>If you haven't yet explored **MORTAL DOWNER**, the **MOBS** video serves as an excellent entry point into the album's sonic territory. Check out the video on the band's official channels and dig deeper into one of Visual Kei's most influential and uncompromising acts.</p>
