<p>Onmyoza has officially released their first live album as a standalone release in two decades. *Gin'yo Sashimai* dropped today, marking a major milestone for the veteran Visual Kei outfit and capturing the energy of their massive 2025 tour in stunning detail.</p>
<p>The 24-track double album was recorded during the final show of the *2025 Gin'yo Tour* at Hokkaido's Sapporo venue on September 11th, following the September 11th kickoff in Yokohama. The tour supported the band's latest studio album *Gin'yo Gozen*, their first original full-length in two and a half years. For those unfamiliar with Onmyoza: they're one of Japan's most theatrical and conceptual Visual Kei bands, known for their elaborate stage productions, Japanese folklore themes, and genre-blending approach that fuses rock with traditional instrumentation.</p>
<p>The last time Onmyoza released a standalone live album was *Onmyo Raibu* back in 2006—a 20-year gap that makes this release feel genuinely special. The setlist reads like a journey through their catalog, spanning from the mystical opener "Mei" through epic tracks like "鈴鹿御前 - 鬼式" (Suzuka Gozen - Oni Form) and closing statement "悪路王" (Akuro-O).</p>
<p>**Bonus Content & Campaigns**</p>
<p>To celebrate the release, Onmyoza is running a live photo panel exhibition at select Tower Records and Joshin stores (March 17-30), with chances to win displayed panels with any purchase. There's also a back catalog campaign offering exclusive wooden coasters at participating retailers.</p>
<p>**Radio Special Incoming**</p>
<p>Here's the real treat: Onmyoza's beloved radio show *Onmyoza Kuroneko no Nekomamma Radio* is making a one-night comeback. On **March 31st at 1:00 AM (TOKYO FM)**, the band will host a special broadcast celebrating the live album's release. Vocalist Kuroneko promises behind-the-scenes stories and rare insights, with all band members appearing together. Listeners can submit messages via the official website.</p>
<p>*Gin'yo Sashimai* retails for ¥4,800 and includes numerous retailer-exclusive bonuses, from stickers to leather bookmarks. Grab it now and tune in for the radio special later this month.</p>
