# HYDE Appears in Casillero del Diablo Wine CM: "Love and Quality Are Essential for Long-Term Success"

HYDE has been appointed as brand ambassador for the wine "Casillero del Diablo" from Devil's Cellar. A web CM featuring HYDE titled "The Devil Is Proof of Deliciousness" will premiere on the official YouTube channel starting April 28, with TV commercials set to air nationwide beginning May 30.

This initiative is being carried out by Mercian Corporation to commemorate the 30th anniversary of the Japanese release of "Casillero del Diablo," a premium Chilean wine brand produced by Concha y Toro, Chile's No. 1 winery. The CM features "SO DREAMY" from HYDE's latest album *JEKYLL*, marking his 25th anniversary as a solo artist.

"Casillero del Diablo" carries a legendary backstory: the wine was so delicious that people would steal it from the cellar. To protect the wine, the winery's founder, Don Melchor, started a rumor that "a devil lives in this cellar." Since then, the "devil mark" has become a symbol of deliciousness cherished worldwide.

The new CM begins with HYDE's narration set against a deep red backdrop. The footage carefully captures the sound of wine being poured and the gesture of tilting a glass, creating an immersive feeling as if one is tasting the wine firsthand. The final product conveys that "deliciousness that gets stolen" that makes you reach for just one more sip. A key visual will be unveiled on May 1, and a collaboration campaign with HYDE will launch on June 6, the "Devil's Day," offering various experiences centered around the brand's iconic "devil mark."

HYDE, who has considerable knowledge of wine, commented on why "Casillero del Diablo" has remained beloved, saying it comes down to "love and quality." Regarding the new CM, he offered this message: "There are scenes with smiles that are unfamiliar for me—I wonder if I did them well? I'm looking forward to hearing what everyone thinks." In a post-shoot interview, HYDE, now marking his 25th year as a solo artist, discusses what he treasures while maintaining his career longevity, a touching episode that stole his heart, and his own "devilish side."

## "SO DREAMY" Is About Sharing Dinner with Someone Special—The Song Features Wine in Its Lyrics, Making It Perfect for This CM

**Q: Were there any particular scenes or episodes from the CM shoot that left a strong impression on you?**

HYDE: The drinking scenes, I'd say. While filming those scenes, I was thinking things like "Cheers!" and "This is delicious!" in my mind. Without saying the words out loud, it's harder to create the right facial expression. So I was mentally saying things like "this is delicious" while filming.

**Q: Your latest album track "SO DREAMY" has been chosen as the CM song, set to release in May. After actually filming, did you feel any common ground or good chemistry between the CM's world and the song?**

HYDE: "SO DREAMY" is also about sharing dinner with someone special. Wine appears in the lyrics too, so it's really the perfect song to have playing in the background of this CM.

**Q: What were your thoughts and impressions when you tasted "Casillero del Diablo"?**

HYDE: It's a really well-known wine in Japan. I discovered it around the time when Chilean wines were becoming a hot topic, and I was surprised—"This wine is this delicious at this price point?" It was quite an eye-opener.

**Q: How do you typically enjoy wine in your everyday life?**

HYDE: I basically drink wine paired with meals, so I love both red and white equally. I also have opportunities to drink after dinner—pairing it with cheese, for example.

**Q: For wine beginners, how would you recommend enjoying wine?**

HYDE: I'd encourage people to choose wine carefully and pair it with food. For example, rich meat dishes go well with red wine, while lighter dishes or fish pair better with white wine. That kind of pairing is the most effective way to enjoy wine deliciously, I think.

**Q: "Casillero del Diablo" is celebrating its 30th anniversary since its Japanese release. What are your honest thoughts, and do you have a congratulatory message?**

HYDE: Thirty years is longer than I initially thought it would be. To sustain something for that long, you need genuine deliciousness and quality. I've actually visited their winery, and it's a truly wonderful place. I got the impression that "they're making wine with love." That's probably why everyone loves it and why it's been able to continue for decades.

**Q: What do you think are the conditions for a wine brand to remain beloved for such a long time?**

HYDE: It really comes down to love, doesn't it? Whether or not proper love and care are being put in. Wine-making has good years and bad years, but I think that's something that can be compensated for based on accumulated experience. Long-established brands have the knowledge to navigate challenges skillfully. Brands without that foundation tend to be more vulnerable to a single setback. I'm sure "Casillero del Diablo" will face various challenges in the future, but I think it will continue thriving. Brands that consistently maintain quality while putting love into each product are strong.

**Q: You yourself are also marking a milestone—your 25th anniversary as a solo artist—and you continue to be active at the forefront of the industry. What do you prioritize to sustain your career long-term?**

HYDE: I think it really comes down to quality. Creating quality that won't sadden or disappoint fans—that's my way of expressing love to them. It's very similar to wine, but avoiding betrayal is crucial.

**Q: Over the long years, there must have been pressures and difficulties that come with longevity. What aspects have been challenging, and how have you overcome them?**

HYDE: I think it's because we've built a good relationship of trust with fans. I've accumulated confidence that "if I make something carefully and earnestly, it will properly reach the fans." So there's no pressure, really. If I have confidence that "I've made this properly," that's perfect for me—a perfect 100. But if there's any shadow of doubt—"Is this okay?"—the fans will see through it. So it's important not to do that.

**Q: Is there anything new you'd like to challenge yourself with in the future?**

HYDE: Just like I have been, I want to continue pursuing the art I believe in without playing it safe. I think that's how I express my love to the fans. I want to maintain that same stance going forward.

**Q: The CM has this striking phrase about "deliciousness that gets stolen." Have you had any experiences where your heart was similarly "stolen" by something deeply moving?**

HYDE: There are many, but for example, when I went to Chile, there was an impressive trouble during a concert. The guitars and drums simply stopped working. It seemed hopeless—like the concert would have to end. But then the microphone was still working. I said, "I'm sorry, I can't perform anymore. But please listen to me sing acapella," and I started singing without accompaniment. It turned into a massive sing-along. It became an incredibly moving live performance. It's partly the nature of Chilean people, but it was a moment where our mutual care and feelings became one.

**Q: The phrase "the legendary devil's wine" is also striking. If you had to point out a "devilish side" within yourself, what would it be?**

HYDE: I have a weakness for the tears of fans crying during concerts—whether they're moved to tears or lost in the lyrics and crying. (laughs) It's kind of cute, you know? While I do feel a bit sorry that they're crying, it's definitely my weakness. I find it adorable.

**Q: Please share a message for everyone watching the CM.**

HYDE: I'm HYDE. I had the opportunity to appear in this CM. There are scenes with smiles that are unfamiliar for me—I wonder if I did them well? I'm looking forward to hearing what everyone thinks!
