If you've been waiting for a chance to catch some of Japan's most distinctive visual kei voices outside the country, this one's for you. Seth from Moi dix Mois, KOUKI from D=OUT, and TSUKASA from THE MICRO HEAD 4N'S are bringing their collaborative unit Sakura Zensen (literally "Cherry Blossom Front") to London—and yes, this is really happening.

For those unfamiliar with these artists: Seth has been the driving force behind Moi dix Mois since the mid-90s, steering the band through countless lineup changes while maintaining their signature gothic, symphonic sound. D=OUT, meanwhile, represents a harder edge of the visual kei spectrum, with KOUKI's contributions helping shape their distinctive blend of heavy instrumentation and theatrical presentation. TSUKASA brings a completely different energy from THE MICRO HEAD 4N'S, whose experimental and avant-garde approach has always set them apart from more traditional VK acts.

Sakura Zensen itself is a special project that brings these three musicians together, offering something unique that you won't get from their respective bands' regular performances. It's the kind of collaboration that rewards dedicated fans who follow multiple acts across Japan's visual kei landscape.

This London appearance is a genuine rarity for Western fans—international live dates for Japanese visual kei acts remain relatively uncommon, especially when you're talking about artists with the pedigree and career longevity of these three. Whether you're a longtime follower of any of these musicians or simply curious about a side of visual kei you might not have encountered before, this is worth your attention.

For specific details about dates, venue, ticket information, and any additional scheduling, check the official channels for each artist and Sakura Zensen directly. These projects typically announce full tour details through their primary social media accounts and fan club channels, so keep an eye on their announcements in the coming weeks.
