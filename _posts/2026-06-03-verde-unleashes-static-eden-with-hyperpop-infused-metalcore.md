Verde/ is pushing into bold new territory with "Static Eden," a devastating single that merges the band's signature post-hardcore and metalcore attack with hyperpop elements for maximum sonic impact. Released today, the track arrives alongside a striking music video that captures the duality embedded in its title—the beauty and destruction inherent in a frozen paradise.

For those new to Verde/, the band has been carving out a reputation for uncompromising heaviness wrapped in poetic, cyberpunk-influenced lyricism courtesy of vocalist Shou. This latest offering represents a marked evolution: the instrumentation is simultaneously precise and abrasive, with the new hyperpop influence adding an unexpected textural dimension that prevents the song from settling into predictable territory.

![Verde/ Static Eden music video still](https://images.weserv.nl/?url=http%3A%2F%2Fvijuttoke.com%2Fcontents%2Fwp-content%2Fuploads%2FSE_sam_low-580x326.jpg&w=800&output=jpg)

The accompanying visual unfolds as a fragmented journey through neon-soaked urban isolation, juxtaposing cold, metallic cityscapes with blinding flashes of surreality. It's a fitting translation of "Static Eden"—the concept of beauty found in stillness, obsession with the unchanging, and the raw impulses lurking beneath the surface.

Verde/ is celebrating the release with a two-part show at Shimokitazawa Shangri-La on June 5th. The first part offers their inaugural free live ("First Free GIG"), providing an accessible entry point to experience their current sonic direction. The second half transforms into a full-scale release party, "Decoding Eden," which will explore the "Static Eden" universe in greater depth while building on momentum from their recent "Spring Circuit: Pulse / Static Eden" tour.

The single is available now across all major streaming platforms and through physical retail, with a pop-up event running through June 14th at Ultra Shibuya featuring live photos, vocalist Shou's worn stage costumes, and exclusive merchandise.
