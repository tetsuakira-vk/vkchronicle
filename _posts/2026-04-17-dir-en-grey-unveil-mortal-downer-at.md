<p>Dir En Grey have launched their 2026 tour cycle in characteristically enigmatic fashion, debuting new material from their 12th studio album *Mortal Downer* at an exclusive fan-club-only show at Club Citta' on April 15th. The performance marked the opening night of ＜TOUR26 Downer Absolutely No One＞ and offered early glimpses into the album's unsettling sonic and visual world—one that has already sparked unusually polarized reactions across social media since the record's April 8th release.</p>
<p>**About Dir En Grey:** One of Japan's most influential visual rock acts, Dir En Grey have spent two decades crafting a signature sound that blends extreme metal, alternative rock, and avant-garde experimentation with theatrical aesthetics and conceptual depth. Vocalist Kyo and guitarist Toshio lead the band through constantly evolving artistic territories.</p>
<p>The album itself has generated fascinatingly contradictory responses: some listeners call it "heavy," others "oppressive"; some find it "accessible," others "difficult to parse." This schism has become part of the work's mystique. The 100-minute setlist drew nine of the album's 14 tracks, including the previously-released single "The Devil In Me" alongside fan favorites from their catalogue, strategically sequenced to deepen (and complicate) interpretations of *Mortal Downer*'s thematic core.</p>
<p>Staging-wise, Dir En Grey pushed further into cinematic territory: the video design carries an unsettling new texture, while lighting effects deployed unprecedented speed and dimensionality. Notably, vocalist Kyo's appearance has already shifted from the band's latest official photographs—though whether this visual evolution persists across the entire tour remains unclear.</p>
<p>The band's momentum remains undeniable: *Mortal Downer* charted at #4 on Japan's Oricon weekly album rankings, extending their streak of consecutive Top 10 albums.</p>
<p>**What's Next:**</p>
<p>The tour continues through May 23rd across major Japanese venues (dates listed below); most early dates have sold out. Two climactic nights at Tokyo Garden Theatre on July 18–19 bear the album's title and represent the true culmination of this cycle. Album purchasers can access presale tickets through April 19th.</p>
