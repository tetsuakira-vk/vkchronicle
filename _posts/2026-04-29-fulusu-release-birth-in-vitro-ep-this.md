<p>fulusu is gearing up to deliver fresh sonic territory with their upcoming EP *Birth in Vitro*, arriving **April 29, 2026**. The four-piece continues to carve out their identity within Japan's underground rock scene with a release that promises introspection and experimental edge.</p>
<p>The Tokyo-based outfit—fronted by the distinctive vocals and guitar work of Joseph Gen, alongside guitarist Yuto Yoneya, bassist Shunsuke Sato, and drummer Sho Sato—has been building momentum with their theatrical approach to rock music. While fulusu operates within the broader Japanese rock ecosystem, their aesthetic sensibilities and sonic ambition position them as artists worth tracking for fans who appreciate the boundary-pushing energy of contemporary J-rock.</p>
<p>**What to Expect**</p>
<p>The EP spans four tracks, each title hinting at the conceptual weight behind this release:</p>
<p>1. Vessel</p>
<p>2. In vitro</p>
<p>3. Pseudo-Life (擬生)</p>
<p>4. Glass Dahlia (硝子のダリア)</p>
<p>The thematic through-line appears to explore artificial creation and mortality—fitting imagery for a band unafraid to embrace darker, more cerebral textures in their songwriting. The title track "In vitro" suggests biomechanical or synthetic life, a concept that aligns with the band's willingness to blend heavy instrumentation with atmospheric production.</p>
<p>**Where to Listen**</p>
<p>The EP will be available across all major streaming platforms via the Friendship release network. You can pre-save and pre-order *Birth in Vitro* through the [official link](https://friendship.lnk.to/BirthinVitro_fulusu).</p>
<p>For Western fans still discovering the wider landscape of Japanese underground rock, fulusu represents the kind of innovative spirit that keeps the scene vibrant—ambitious without sacrificing musicianship, and theatrical without relying on established tropes.</p>
<p>Mark your calendars for April 29th, and dive into *Birth in Vitro* when it drops.</p>
