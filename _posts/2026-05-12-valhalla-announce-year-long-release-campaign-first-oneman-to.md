VALHALLA are ramping up their presence in a major way. The Japanese Visual Kei outfit has unveiled an ambitious 12-month release schedule spanning multiple singles and two new mini-albums, while simultaneously announcing their first-ever oneman tour, titled *-Ascension-*.

For those new to the band, VALHALLA bring a theatrical, symphonic edge to the Visual Kei landscape, blending orchestral arrangements with heavy instrumentation and dramatic vocal work. They've steadily built momentum since their formation, and this announcement signals a significant step forward in their career trajectory.

The release campaign is spread across the coming year, giving fans a steady stream of new material to anticipate. Rather than dropping everything at once, the band has strategically paced their output—a smart move that keeps listener engagement high and builds momentum toward each release. The two mini-albums represent substantial projects beyond the single rollouts, suggesting VALHALLA are diversifying their sonic palette and perhaps experimenting with different concepts or themes.

What makes this news particularly exciting is the concurrent oneman tour. *-Ascension-* signals the band's confidence in their fanbase and their readiness to headline their own shows across Japan. Oneman tours are a crucial milestone for Visual Kei bands; they demonstrate both popularity and artistic maturity. Fans attending these shows can expect to hear new material live alongside fan favorites, creating a unique snapshot of the band's evolution.

For Western fans, this is a pivotal moment to jump in. The combination of fresh releases and a dedicated tour means there's never been a better time to explore VALHALLA's discography or catch them live if you're traveling to Japan.

Keep your eyes on VALHALLA's official channels for tour dates, venue information, and ticket availability.
