Visual Kei veterans PLASTIC are gearing up for a busy summer with back-to-back live announcements that should excite longtime fans of the band's theatrical, experimental sound.

For those unfamiliar with PLASTIC, the group emerged in the mid-2000s as part of the Visual Kei underground scene, known for blending gothic aesthetics with progressive rock sensibilities. The band's lineup has cycled through members over the years, but they've maintained a dedicated following thanks to their intense live performances and genre-defying approach to composition.

The band has just confirmed two major live dates for July 2025. On July 13, PLASTIC will perform at Nagoya's CLAPPER venue, while a week later on July 20, they'll take the stage at an unspecified Hearts+ location. Both dates promise to showcase the band's signature energy and intricate musicianship.

This announcement marks a significant moment for PLASTIC as they approach their 25th anniversary, a milestone that underscores their longevity in a scene where many bands burn bright and fade quickly. After a relatively quieter period in the mid-2010s, the band's return to active touring signals renewed momentum—and Western fans have increasingly taken notice of PLASTIC's influence on contemporary Visual Kei acts.

The original PLASTIC lineup featured vocalist kaim, guitarist Masayuki Ishii, bassist Yu Mizuguchi, and drummer Yasutomo Tsukioka. While roster changes have occurred (notably with various collaborative projects), the core energy that defined their early work remains intact.

Ticket details and venue information for both shows are still being finalized, but fans should keep an eye on VKDB and PLASTIC's official channels for updates. Given the band's reputation for innovative live arrangements and theatrical stage presence, these performances could be essential viewing for dedicated Visual Kei enthusiasts.
