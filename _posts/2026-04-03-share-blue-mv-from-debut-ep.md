<p>お風呂と街灯 (Ofuro to Gaito) are making waves with their debut EP, and the "BLUE" music video is a visceral statement of intent.</p>
<p>For those unfamiliar with the band, お風呂と街灯 represent a new generation of Japanese artists blending theatrical sensibilities with raw emotional intensity—hallmarks of Visual Kei's evolving landscape. The group has been building momentum as they transition into full band mode, and this first EP marks a significant milestone in their career.</p>
<p>The "BLUE" music video, directed by Hakua Oe, is impossible to ignore. It opens with a haunting premise: the band's members spot an abandoned upright piano in the bed of a pickup truck. What follows is a desperate chase—the piano symbolizes identity itself, and watching them cling to it as the truck lurches forward creates an unforgettable image of raw longing and refusal to let go.</p>
<p>Musically, "BLUE" walks a tightrope between delicate and destructive. The production features Romantic (known for his work with 爆弾ジョニー and as a keyboardist on betcover!!) as producer, bringing a polished yet emotionally unguarded sensibility. The song sits in mid-tempo territory, anchored by an oddly hypnotic melodic hook that lingers long after the first listen. The vocals are transparent yet distinctly textured—a perfect match for arrangements that feel both introspective and urgent.</p>
<p>The band describes the *Dawning/Window* EP as "rain falling equally on all those carrying feelings with nowhere to go." It's thematically cohesive and philosophically ambitious—the kind of artistic statement that suggests お風呂と街灯 aren't interested in easy answers or safe aesthetics.</p>
<p>The digital EP landed February 18, 2026. If you want to experience this in person, the band's first headline shows are coming in May: **May 22 in Osaka** (Pangea) and **May 25 in Tokyo** (Basement Bar). Tickets are available through the official link below.</p>
<p>Stream *Dawning/Window* now and don't sleep on these artists.</p>
