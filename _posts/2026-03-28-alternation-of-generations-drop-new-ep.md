<p>Japanese Visual Kei outfit Alternation of Generations is gearing up to expand their discography with a fresh six-track EP that promises to showcase the band's evolving sonic palette.</p>
<p>**REVOLVE** arrives on April 22, 2026, priced at ¥2,900. The release lands as a solid mid-length project that gives fans a solid glimpse into where the band is headed creatively. For those unfamiliar with Alternation of Generations, the group has carved out a dedicated following within the VK underground thanks to their theatrical presentation, melodic sensibilities, and willingness to experiment across their releases.</p>
<p>The tracklist reads as follows:</p>
<p>1. REVOLVE</p>
<p>2. THE M＠D SHOW</p>
<p>3. Day After Day</p>
<p>4. ルミナス (Luminous)</p>
<p>5. Lost Child</p>
<p>6. Grateful for Everything</p>
<p>What immediately stands out is the mix of English and Japanese track titles—a common thread in contemporary Visual Kei that reflects the genre's appeal to both domestic and international audiences. The opening title track sets the thematic tone, while standouts like "THE M＠D SHOW" (with its quirky "@" symbol) suggest the band isn't taking themselves too seriously. The inclusion of "ルミナス" (Luminous) signals a potential shift toward more introspective, possibly atmospheric territory, contrasting nicely with what sounds like heavier moments elsewhere on the EP.</p>
<p>**REVOLVE** comes at an interesting time for the Visual Kei scene, where bands continue to balance accessibility with the theatrical extremism that defines the genre. At under 30 minutes, this EP functions as a perfect entry point for newcomers while rewarding longtime fans with deeper cuts that showcase the band's range.</p>
<p>If you've been sleeping on Alternation of Generations, **REVOLVE** is your chance to jump in. Pre-orders should go live soon—keep an eye on Japanese music retailers and the band's official channels for details. This is Visual Kei evolution in real time.</p>
