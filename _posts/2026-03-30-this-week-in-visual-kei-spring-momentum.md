<p>What a week to be a Visual Kei fan. We're officially in that sweet spot where spring releases are dropping, tours are being announced left and right, and the scene feels genuinely alive with possibility. From legacy acts celebrating milestones to fresh blood making their debut, this has been *the* kind of week that reminds us why we're obsessed with this genre.</p>
<p>**New Releases Everywhere**</p>
<p>The release calendar is absolutely packed. UNiTE. are gearing up for a major best album moment with "HOPES #3" arriving in July, while KIRITO (Angelo) is dropping the "SHIFT" mini-album in April. If you want something to spin *right now*, GOTCHAROCKA just unleashed the double A-side "0 MINUS 0 / Wicked Div.1," and PaleNeØ are making waves with their second maxi-single "UNISØN." DIR EN GREY continue their "MORTAL DOWNER" campaign with fresh visuals that hit different, and—because we can't talk about new music without mentioning them—the FUZZ's album "生命の唄" is locked in for June 2026.</p>
<p>**Anniversaries, Reunions & Comebacks**</p>
<p>The nostalgic energy is real this week. KALEN are doing a one-day revival show for their 20th anniversary (yes, *that* KALEN), while YONA YONA WEEKENDERS celebrated hitting the 10-year mark with "Tokio Dungeon." Re:name just dropped the album '1626' to mark a decade together, and—in a genuinely heartwarming moment—Sick. announced their return after a 7-year hiatus with a new lineup in tow. EPHEM, meanwhile, are brand new to the scene entirely, with their first live already on the horizon.</p>
<p>**Tours & Live Energy**</p>
<p>Eve announced their first live house tour in 7 years, which feels like a big deal for anyone who's been following them. And if you want a taste of live VK chaos, ZORO's recent Club Malcolm show was absolute pandemonium—40+ repeats of one track will do that, apparently.</p>
<p>**New Faces**</p>
<p>ELISE and OPHELIA both dropped fresh visuals this week, proving the new wave of VK bands isn't messing around with their aesthetics. Whether it's ELISE's elegant approach on "SATELLITE" or OPHELIA's latest single visual, these bands are reminding us why we're always hunting for the next sound.</p>
<p>Keep your eyes peeled next week for more April releases and tour announcements—the momentum isn't slowing down.</p>
