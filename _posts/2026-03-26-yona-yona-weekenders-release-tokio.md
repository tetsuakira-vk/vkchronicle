<p>YONA YONA WEEKENDERS are kicking off their 10th anniversary year in style with the release of "Tokio Dungeon," a groove-laden new single that showcases the band's evolution while honoring their signature sound.</p>
<p>The track arrives today across all digital platforms, accompanied by a striking lyric video that captures the band's dynamic visual aesthetic. Built around a jungle-influenced beat, "Tokio Dungeon" channels the chaotic energy of urban life—breathing in the discord and sensory overload of the city itself. It's a departure that still feels distinctly YONA YONA WEEKENDERS: thoughtfully crafted "good music for chewing on," as the band describes their philosophy, but with a fresh edge that marks a new chapter.</p>
<p>For those unfamiliar with the Tokyo outfit, YONA YONA WEEKENDERS have carved out a distinctive niche in the Japanese rock underground since their formation, blending theatrical presentation with alternative rock sensibilities. Their approach to Visual Kei aesthetics emphasizes atmosphere and sound as much as visual spectacle—a band that rewards close listening.</p>
<p>The lyric video itself deserves attention. Shot on an industrial staircase, the footage captures the band ascending and descending, performing amid the concrete geometry. It's a meditation on modern isolation: simultaneous moments of daily life happening everywhere at once, people living through the same night without ever intersecting. It's simultaneously chaotic and melancholic—perfectly aligned with the song's sonic landscape.</p>
<p>The anniversary push continues beyond the single. YONA YONA WEEKENDERS will headline two "YONANOHI" oneman live shows in 2026:</p>
<p>**Tokyo** – April 7 at EX THEATER ROPPONGI (¥5,500 + 1 drink)</p>
<p>**Taipei** – April 25 at The Wall Live House</p>
<p>Tickets for both dates are on sale now via the links below.</p>
<p>If you've been sleeping on YONA YONA WEEKENDERS, "Tokio Dungeon" is an excellent entry point. Check out the lyric video on YouTube, grab the single, and consider catching them live if you're in Asia this spring.</p>
