We're catching up on two months of fresh Visual Kei material in one go—and honestly, it's the perfect excuse to dive deep into what the scene has been cooking up while we've been dealing with life's inevitable curveballs.

April's new releases playlist got shelved due to the usual suspects: travel schedules, unexpected illness, and the kind of forgetfulness that happens when you're juggling too many projects at once. But rather than pretend those weeks never happened, we're rolling both April and May together into one comprehensive feature that showcases the breadth of what Japan's underground rock scene has delivered across eight weeks.

This double-feature format gives us breathing room to highlight tracks and projects that might have slipped past the radar for Western fans during the spring months. Whether you're tracking major label announcements from established acts or hunting for hidden gems from emerging bands, there's usually something worth your attention in any given month—you just need someone to curate it.

The Visual Kei landscape in 2026 continues to reflect the genre's evolution: some bands are experimenting with production techniques that push traditional instrumentation into unexpected sonic territory, while others are doubling down on the raw energy that made the scene essential in the first place. The diversity is part of what keeps the community engaged and constantly discovering new favorites.

If you've been following along with our monthly roundups, you know the drill. We're pulling from across the spectrum—major label releases, independent drops, limited edition physical media, and digital-only singles that deserve attention. This combined playlist serves as a snapshot of two months of creative output that collectively remind us why Visual Kei remains one of Japan's most vital and visually striking music scenes.
