<p>A fresh chapter in the Visual Kei underground is unfolding with the emergence of ラルン -LA LUNE- (La Lune), a new project that brings together members from beloved disbanded acts. The band has announced their debut digital single, "今宵、リリスで逢いましょう" (Tonight, Let's Meet at Lilith), set for release on March 20, 2026.</p>
<p>La Lune features LUNA on vocals, formerly of the theatrical Visual Kei act Bedtime Story, whose haunting vocal delivery defined much of that project's mystique. Anchoring the rhythm section is 雪兎 (Yukito) on bass, previously active in MAD Night—a name familiar to those who followed the deeper corners of Japan's underground rock scene. This pairing of seasoned musicians signals serious intent; both members bring years of experience navigating the demands of VK's elaborate live performances and intricate compositions.</p>
<p>The single's title, "Tonight, Let's Meet at Lilith," carries an intriguing mystique befitting the project's aesthetic. With a runtime of approximately 4 minutes according to the TuneCore Japan listing, the track promises to showcase the band's sonic direction without requiring a full album commitment—a smart move for a debut release in today's digital landscape.</p>
<p>For Western fans accustomed to discovering VK acts through underground forums and dedicated communities, La Lune represents exactly the kind of grassroots project that keeps the scene vital. The involvement of ex-members from defunct bands demonstrates that the Visual Kei spirit persists through recombination and reinvention, rather than dissolution.</p>
<p>The single will be available through TuneCore Japan and major digital platforms. If you've followed Bedtime Story's theatrical darkness or tracked MAD Night's underground presence, La Lune deserves your attention. Mark March 20, 2026, on your calendar, and keep an eye on their Twitter (@10lalune01) for additional updates.</p>
