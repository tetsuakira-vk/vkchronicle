<p>Versailles is heading to Latin America this December, and it's a huge deal for the sophisticated visual kei outfit. The band—known for their ornate costumes, classical-inspired arrangements, and theatrical presence—will hit both Mexico and Chile as part of an expanded touring schedule.</p>
<p>First up is a solo live in Santiago, Chile on **December 3rd at Teatro Coliseo**. This marks the band's first performance in the country in a decade and their first headlining show in three years. Tickets go on sale **March 27 at 11:00** via Punto Ticket. For fans in the region who've been waiting for Versailles to return, this is unmissable.</p>
<p>Two days later, the band will perform at **KNOTFEST 2026** on **December 5th at Estadio Fray Nano in Mexico City**—Slipknot's flagship metal festival. Tickets drop **March 23 at 11:00** on FunTicket MX. Versailles joining a festival lineup alongside one of metal's biggest names underscores how far visual kei has penetrated the international metal consciousness.</p>
<p>But the year kicks off earlier in Europe. Versailles will appear at **B7KLAN J-ROCK FEST** in Paris on **July 11–12 at Arena Grand Paris**—tickets are already available.</p>
<p>Shortly after, frontman **KAMIJO** will make his signature U.S. debut with a solo show called **SANCTUS OF THE DAMNED** on **July 18th at The United Theater on Broadway in Los Angeles, California**. This marks a major milestone for the vocalist, who has steadily built a devoted Western fanbase.</p>
<p>For Western fans, these announcements represent genuine accessibility: Versailles touring North America and South America isn't routine, and their return to Chile after a decade-long absence signals serious commitment to expanding their global presence. Whether you catch them at a festival or their intimate solo show, expect the ornate visuals, intricate musicianship, and theatrical presence Versailles are renowned for.</p>
<p>Mark your calendars and grab tickets while you can—opportunities like this don't come around often for visual kei acts in the Americas.</p>
