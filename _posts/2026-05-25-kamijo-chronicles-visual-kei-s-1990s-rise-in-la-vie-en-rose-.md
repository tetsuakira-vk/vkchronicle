KAMIJO is taking fans deeper into the foundations of visual kei itself. The LAREINE mastermind and solo artist has released chapter 2 of his serialized project *LA VIE EN ROSE*, which trades in storytelling about the genre's most formative era—1990s Tokyo—and the pivotal moments that birthed the visual kei movement as we know it.

For those less familiar: KAMIJO founded LAREINE in the mid-1990s, a cornerstone neoclassical visual kei act that helped define the genre's aristocratic, gothic aesthetic. The band's theatrical presence and Kamijo's operatic vocals became touchstones for an entire wave of VK acts. Though LAREINE has cycled through hiatuses and reunions over the decades, Kamijo's solo career has flourished, and his influence on modern visual kei remains immense—his recent works continue to push theatrical boundaries while honoring the genre's ornate roots.

*LA VIE EN ROSE* appears to be a retrospective project where Kamijo recounts the encounters and upheavals that shaped not only LAREINE's emergence but the broader ascension of visual kei as a cultural phenomenon. Setting the narrative in 1990s Tokyo grounds the story in a specific place and time: the clubs, the underground scenes, and the creative ferment that allowed visual kei to evolve from fringe experiment into a defining movement in Japanese rock.

Chapter 2 digs into those early encounters and turning points—the collaborations, the creative risks, and the industry shifts that allowed visual kei to grow beyond its initial underground following. For longtime fans, this is essential context. For newer listeners discovering KAMIJO or LAREINE, it's a masterclass in where this theatrical, boundary-pushing aesthetic actually came from.

If you're curious about visual kei's roots or simply want to revisit this pivotal era through the eyes of someone who lived it, keep an eye on KAMIJO's official channels and social media for updates on future chapters. His current solo material and archival releases are widely available on major streaming platforms.
