Three distinctive Visual Kei acts are joining forces for an ambitious nationwide arena tour that promises to be one of summer 2026's most compelling cross-genre events.

Age Factory, ENTH, and Paledusk have announced the second iteration of their collaborative GOBLIN tour, expanding from their initial run into a full-scale Zepp circuit across Japan's five major metropolitan areas. The three bands—each bringing their own sonic identity and visual aesthetic to the table—reportedly bonded through encounters at each other's live shows and festival appearances, leading to this creative partnership.

![Age Factory promotional image](https://images.weserv.nl/?url=https%3A%2F%2Fnew-img.barks.jp%2Fwp-content%2Fuploads%2F2026%2F01%2F09140939%2FAge-Factory2025%25E3%2582%25A2%25E3%2583%25BC%25E5%2586%2599.jpg&w=800&output=jpg)

Age Factory brings their atmospheric yet aggressive approach to the lineup, while ENTH's intricate compositional structures and theatrical presentation add another layer to the bill. Paledusk rounds out the trio with their own distinctive take on modern Visual Kei sensibilities.

![ENTH promotional image](https://images.weserv.nl/?url=https%3A%2F%2Fnew-img.barks.jp%2Fwp-content%2Fuploads%2F2026%2F05%2F19120147%2FENTH_A%25E5%2586%2599.jpg&w=800&output=jpg)

The GOBLIN TOUR 2026 will hit five cities across August and September:

**August 6** — Zepp Sapporo  
**August 17** — Zepp Nagoya  
**August 24** — Zepp Osaka bayside  
**August 27** — Zepp Fukuoka  
**September 2** — Zepp Haneda (Tokyo)

All shows open at 6:00 PM with performances starting at 7:00 PM. Ticket prices are uniform across venues: ¥5,500 (including drinks) for first-floor standing and ¥6,000 (including drinks) for second-floor reserved seating. Official pre-sale closes May 31 at 11:59 PM, so fans should act quickly.

![Paledusk promotional image](https://images.weserv.nl/?url=https%3A%2F%2Fnew-img.barks.jp%2Fwp-content%2Fuploads%2F2026%2F05%2F19120150%2FPaledusk_2026_%25E3%2582%25A2%25E3%2583%25BC%25E5%2586%2599.jpg&w=800&output=jpg)

This kind of collaborative tour concept—uniting three bands of different stylistic persuasions under one banner—has become increasingly important in the modern Visual Kei landscape, fostering community and introducing fans to artists they might not have discovered otherwise.
