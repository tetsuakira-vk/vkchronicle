The countdown is officially on for one of 2026's most intriguing Visual Kei arrivals. Fatüm—a gothic unit featuring SUI (formerly of DAVID) and guitarist HIDE-ZOU (D)—has just opened its official website and is preparing to make a stunning live debut in mere days.

For those unfamiliar with the project, Fatüm represents a deliberate collaboration between two established musicians stepping into fresh creative territory. The duo has already built intrigue through released visuals, with a newly-unveiled MV giving fans their first real taste of the "Magia Fartha Gothic" aesthetic they're building. It's a sound and presentation steeped in theatrical darkness—exactly the kind of ambitious, concept-driven work that defines the best of modern Visual Kei.

The freshly-launched official website isn't just a digital storefront; it includes a comprehensive founding interview as a special feature, offering deeper insight into the band's vision and the creative forces driving them forward. For Western fans still getting to know the project, this is essential reading before the live debut.

Speaking of which: Fatüm's inaugural showcase, titled "Vox Dei," takes place on **May 30, 2026 at Shibuya REX** in Tokyo. The bill includes support from bassist MASASHI (Versailles) and drummer Hibiki (Mantoku Opera), two seasoned professionals who'll anchor the live sound impressively.

Ticket tiers range from 4,500 yen (advance) to 10,000 yen (VIP), with VIP bundles including exclusive merchandise and a two-shot instant photo. An after-talk event with SUI and HIDE-ZOU follows the main show at 17:30 (dress code: jade-colored items required).

Front-row seats are moving fast. Tickets are available now through the official web shop, with advance allocation nearly sold out.

If you've been curious about where two seasoned Visual Kei musicians are headed next, this is the moment to pay attention. Hit the band's official channels—Instagram, X, and the newly-opened website—for all current details and remaining ticket availability.
