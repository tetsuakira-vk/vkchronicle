<p>Arlequin is bringing a special nostalgic moment to one of Tokyo's most storied Visual Kei venues. The band will headline the Ikebukuro BlackHole 17th Anniversary celebration with a unique two-day gender-separated live event.</p>
<p>For those unfamiliar with Arlequin, the band carries deep roots with BlackHole itself—they previously performed at the legendary venue under the name "blind" before their official formation. This anniversary show feels like a full-circle moment, with the event itself titled *blind* as a direct callback to that history.</p>
<p>The celebration takes place **June 19–20, 2026** at Ikebukuro BlackHole. Day One (June 19) marks the first women-only show at the venue in two years, while Day Two (June 20) continues BlackHole's traditional men-only live format—a long-standing tradition that many Japanese VK venues maintain to create distinct audience experiences.</p>
<p>**Event Details:**</p>
<p>**June 19 (Friday) – Ladies Only**</p>
<p>- Doors: 6:30 PM | Start: 7:00 PM</p>
<p>**June 20 (Saturday) – Men's Only**</p>
<p>- Doors: 4:30 PM | Start: 5:00 PM</p>
<p>**Venue:** Ikebukuro BlackHole</p>
<p>This is essentially a one-band headline event across both days, making it a significant moment for Arlequin fans. The gender-specific format is classic to Japanese live house culture and creates an interesting dynamic—if you haven't experienced a single-gender VK live, this is a rare opportunity to understand why these events hold such meaning in the scene.</p>
<p>For more details and ticket information, check out **Arlequin's official website** at arlequin-web.com/news/4829.html. You can also stay updated via their **Instagram** (@arlequin_offi) and **X/Twitter** (@Arlequin_off).</p>
<p>Mark your calendars if you're in Tokyo—BlackHole 17 years strong, and Arlequin's connection to the venue makes this a must-see for VK enthusiasts.</p>
