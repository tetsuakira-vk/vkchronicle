<p>Rising Visual Kei trio HUMAN ERROR 2 is gearing up for an exciting spring with the simultaneous release of two new digital singles this April. The band—featuring vocalist 大宰 (Dazai), guitarist ナツメ (Natsume), and bassist acta—will drop "幸福人間" (Kōfuku Ningen / Happy Human) and "hate" on all major streaming platforms on April 12, 2026.</p>
<p>Alongside the digital release, the band will unveil a brand-new music video and visual imagery for "幸福人間" on the same day, giving fans a full multimedia experience to sink their teeth into. This dual-single approach showcases HUMAN ERROR 2's willingness to explore contrasting sonic and thematic territory—pairing what appears to be an ironically titled uplifting track with a stark, aggressive anthem.</p>
<p>For those unfamiliar with the band, HUMAN ERROR 2 represents a fresh voice in the contemporary Visual Kei underground, blending theatrical presentation with visceral rock instrumentation. The group has been steadily building momentum within the Japanese music scene, and these new releases signal their intent to expand their reach.</p>
<p>The timing of this announcement also comes with exciting live news. HUMAN ERROR 2 will headline a one-man show at Shinjuku HEIST on May 25, 2026—giving Western fans and Tokyo-based listeners a prime opportunity to experience the band's stage presence firsthand. For those planning a Japan trip or already based in the region, this is a must-catch performance from an emerging VK act.</p>
<p>Fans can stream "幸福人間" and "hate" when they drop on April 12 via Spotify, Apple Music, YouTube Music, and other major platforms. Keep an eye on the band's Twitter (@human_error0130) for further updates on the music video release and live show details.</p>
<p>If you've been sleeping on HUMAN ERROR 2, now's the perfect time to jump in. Mark your calendars for April 12—and if you're in Tokyo in late May, secure your tickets to witness their one-man live.</p>
