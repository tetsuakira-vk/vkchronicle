<p>Japanese Visual Kei band Empress is ramping up the intensity with a brand new EP that's primed to deliver dark, aggressive energy. The four-piece has announced the April 29, 2026 release of **BERSERK**, a five-track EP that showcases the band's signature theatrical intensity and polished musicianship.</p>
<p>For those unfamiliar, Empress is a female-fronted Visual Kei outfit led by vocalist LeA, alongside guitarist Mitsuki, bassist Marie, and drummer Iori. The band has carved out a solid reputation in Japan's underground VK scene for their dynamic sound—blending heavy guitar work with atmospheric production and LeA's commanding vocal presence. If you've been following the resurgence of darker, more aggressive Visual Kei in recent years, Empress represents that wave perfectly.</p>
<p>**BERSERK** consists of five tracks: the title track "BERSERK," "Howling," "Days," "Giving," and "Restart." The lead single and music video are already available on YouTube, giving fans a taste of what's to come. From what we can gather from the artwork and the band's aesthetic trajectory, expect the polished production and theatrical presentation you'd want from a modern VK release—no compromises.</p>
<p>The EP's title itself signals the band's intent: *berserk* suggests uncontrolled fury and raw power, which aligns perfectly with the darker end of the Visual Kei spectrum. This isn't introspective emo-rock; this is music designed to hit hard.</p>
<p>Mark your calendars for **April 29, 2026**. If you're into contemporary Visual Kei with genuine heaviness and female-led vocals that cut through the mix, this is essential listening. Check out the music video linked above to get a sense of where Empress are headed, and keep an eye on their Twitter for pre-order details and additional announcements.</p>
