CYNHN just dropped some exciting news at an open-air free live in Tokyo's Yoyogi Park, and the announcements should have fans marking their calendars immediately.

The band premiered their new single "春を攫った" (Haru wo Saratta / Spring Was Stolen) to a packed crowd at the Yoyogi Park outdoor stage today. The track will officially release on June 3, 2026, with pre-adds and pre-saves already available. Based on the track's atmospheric title and the band's theatrical sensibilities, expect something layered and emotionally resonant.

![CYNHN new single artwork](https://images.weserv.nl/?url=https%3A%2F%2Fnew-img.barks.jp%2Fwp-content%2Fuploads%2F2026%2F05%2F13144452%2Fcynhn_haru_jk.jpg&w=800&output=jpg)

For those unfamiliar with CYNHN, the Tokyo-based act has carved out a distinct identity within Japan's visual rock landscape, blending intricate arrangements with evocative vocal work and carefully crafted aesthetics. They've been building momentum steadily since their formation.

But here's the real headline: **CYNHN will headline Zepp DiverCity Tokyo on November 16, 2026** — marking their biggest solo live to date. This is a significant milestone for the group, as Zepp DiverCity represents a major step up in venue size and prestige within Japan's live music circuit. It's the kind of show that separates the rising acts from the established ones.

![CYNHN 9th Anniversary Live announcement](https://images.weserv.nl/?url=https%3A%2F%2Fnew-img.barks.jp%2Fwp-content%2Fuploads%2F2026%2F05%2F13144438%2Fcontent_CYNHN_0629_9th%25E5%2591%258A%25E7%259F%25A5%25E7%2594%25BB%25E5%2583%258F.jpg&w=800&output=jpg)

Before that massive show, CYNHN will also celebrate their 9th anniversary with a live at Shibuya-ku's Liquidroom on June 29, 2026 (doors 6 PM, start 7 PM). That's your chance to catch them in a more intimate setting before they claim the larger stage.

Head over to CYNHN's official website, X, Instagram, and YouTube channel for the latest updates, ticket information, and streaming links for "春を攫った" when it drops in June. This is a band worth keeping on your radar.
