<p>After a hiatus, Japanese rock act Sadie is officially back in the studio—and they're bringing new material with them. The band has announced their first newly recorded single since their activity resumption, titled "悪の花" (Flower of Evil), set to drop in September 2026.</p>
<p>For those unfamiliar with Sadie, the Tokyo-based rock band emerged in the early 2000s and built a dedicated following with their blend of theatrical rock sensibilities and dark, introspective songwriting. Their aesthetic walks the line between alternative rock and visual rock traditions, marked by moody arrangements and atmospheric production. The announcement of fresh material is a significant moment for longtime supporters who've been waiting to hear what direction the band takes post-reunion.</p>
<p>The single's title alone—"Flower of Evil"—signals a return to the darker thematic territory that defined much of Sadie's earlier work. While the band has kept production details close to the chest for now, the artwork teased on the announcement carries the ominous, gothic aesthetic fans would expect. This marks an important milestone: not a compilation or reissue, but genuine new creative output.</p>
<p>For Western fans, this is a reminder that the Japanese rock underground continues to thrive beyond the mainstream radar. Sadie's return matters because it's proof that bands with smaller but devoted international fanbases are still creating, still recording, and still pushing forward.</p>
<p>The September 2026 release window gives fans plenty of time to prepare, and we'd expect further details—including track listings, potential B-sides, and possibly live date announcements—to surface as we approach the drop date. If you've followed Sadie's journey or are just discovering them now, mark your calendar. This is a release worth paying attention to.</p>
<p>Keep an eye on official channels for pre-order information and additional details as the release date draws closer.</p>
