<p>Two notable Visual Kei figures have joined forces to launch an intriguing new project: **Fatüm**, a band pairing vocalist SUI (formerly of DAVID) with guitarist and vocalist HIDE-ZOU (D), under the creative supervision of Mana from Moi dix Mois.</p>
<p>SUI has long been a recognizable presence in the VK underground, known for theatrical performances and experimental sonic direction. HIDE-ZOU brings his own distinct approach to guitar-driven composition. The involvement of Mana—a legendary figure in Visual Kei history and mastermind behind Moi dix Mois—signals that Fatüm is positioned as a serious artistic endeavor rather than a casual side project. Mana's supervisory role suggests fans can expect conceptually ambitious, visually striking presentations in line with his distinctive aesthetic philosophy.</p>
<p>**What We Know:**</p>
<p>Fatüm's debut showcase, titled **"Vox Dei"** (Voice of God), takes place on **May 30th at Shibuya REX** in Tokyo. The event will feature support from notable musicians: MASASHI from symphonic metal outfit Versailles and Hibiki from theatrical act Matenrou Opera. This stellar lineup hints at the sonic direction—expect elaborate, production-heavy performances.</p>
<p>Ticket sales open via their official webshop on **April 18th at midnight JST**. VIP packages will include an exclusive secret disc, continuing SUI's well-documented tradition of rewarding dedicated fans with special merchandise incentives.</p>
<p>**Where to Follow:**</p>
<p>Keep up with announcements and ticket information on their **X (Twitter) account: @fatuminfo**</p>
<p>This announcement arrives amid renewed interest in underground Visual Kei projects that prioritize artistic integrity over commercial accessibility. With Mana's fingerprints on the project and two respected musicians at its core, Fatüm appears poised to deliver something genuinely compelling. Western fans should mark May 30th and monitor their social channels closely—this is exactly the type of project worth following closely.</p>
