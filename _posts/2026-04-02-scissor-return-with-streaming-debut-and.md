<p>Over two decades after disbanding, SCISSOR are making a surprise return to the spotlight with the digital release of *remember cut*, a 26-track best-of compilation now streaming across all major platforms.</p>
<p>For those unfamiliar with the band, SCISSOR were an experimental Visual Kei outfit from the L∞p・Ash RECORDS roster, known for their theatrical approach and unconventional song structures. Though they've been inactive for years, the label's recent push to digitize its catalog has finally brought SCISSOR's music to streaming services—a victory for Western fans who've struggled to access their work.</p>
<p>*Remember cut* spans the band's entire discography in chronological order, offering newcomers a comprehensive entry point into their sound. The compilation includes fan favorites like "GHOST SHIP ~Nanatsu no Umi no Monogatari~" (Ghost Ship: A Tale of Seven Seas), "Mienai Uwasa" (Invisible Rumors), and "Beautiful Sky," among 26 total tracks.</p>
<p>One note: Track 12, "BLIND MAN," has a production error and cuts off abruptly around the 3-minute mark—something to be aware of if you're collecting the full experience.</p>
<p>The streaming release isn't just about archival. SCISSOR have also unveiled fresh promotional material, signaling that this revival is more than nostalgia—it's a genuine reactivation.</p>
<p>**The Real News: Live Comeback**</p>
<p>Most excitingly, SCISSOR will perform at the **MASK 20th anniversary event** on **May 1st and 2nd, 2026** at **Toyosu Pit** in Tokyo. This isn't a one-off; the band is reforming with their classic lineup, including guitarist GRIF, who's making his return to complete the original five-member configuration.</p>
<p>For Western fans, this is a major moment. SCISSOR represent a particular strain of L∞p・Ash's experimental legacy, and live footage from these reunion shows will finally give international audiences a chance to experience their theatrical vision.</p>
<p>**Don't sleep on this.** Stream *remember cut* now to get up to speed, and mark your calendars if you're planning a Japan trip in May. This is the kind of reunion that rarely happens in Visual Kei.</p>
