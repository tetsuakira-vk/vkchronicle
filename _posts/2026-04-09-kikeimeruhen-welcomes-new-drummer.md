<p>Kikeimeruhen (畸形メるへん) has strengthened its lineup with an exciting addition: drummer Dr. キスト (KIST) officially joined the band on March 22, 2026. The timing couldn't be better, as the group has already unveiled two striking digital singles that showcase the band's darkly theatrical sound.</p>
<p>"呪いのA子" (Curse of A-ko) and "妄想ピエロ" (Delusional Pierrot) dropped on February 9 and February 20, 2026 respectively, giving fans a taste of what's to come from the refreshed lineup. Both tracks are now available on streaming and digital music platforms.</p>
<p>**Meet Kikeimeruhen:**</p>
<p>The band brings together some compelling talent within Japan's underground rock scene:</p>
<p>- **Vocals:** Tatsuya (たつや) — formerly of pyCHEDELICA and Stockholm, bringing experienced vocal prowess to the project</p>
<p>- **Guitar:** Masakiyo (マサキヨ)</p>
<p>- **Bass:** Gazeru (ガゼル)</p>
<p>- **Drums:** KIST (キスト) — the newest member fueling the rhythm section</p>
<p>Tatsuya's background in previous projects hints at Kikeimeruhen's artistic ambitions. The band's aesthetic leans into theatrical, conceptually-driven rock with a distinctly eerie atmosphere — evident from their single titles and visual presentation.</p>
<p>**Check Out the New Releases:**</p>
<p>Both singles are worth your immediate attention. "呪いのA子" is available on YouTube and major digital platforms including BIG UP!, where you can also find "妄想ピエロ." The tracks demonstrate the band's ability to craft memorable hooks within a darker, more experimental framework.</p>
<p>If you're into Japanese rock acts with a theatrical edge and aren't already familiar with Kikeimeruhen, now is the perfect time to dive in. With KIST's arrival and fresh material hitting the digital streets, the band appears poised to make stronger waves in the scene.</p>
<p>Head over to BIG UP! or YouTube to stream both singles and get a feel for where Kikeimeruhen is headed in 2026.</p>
