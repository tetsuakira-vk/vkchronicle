The legendary hellish overlord is back with fresh sonic devastation. Damian Hamada's Creatures, the heavy metal project fronted by the founding member of cult icons Seikima II, will unleash a new digital single titled "Seven Deadly Sins" on July 31, 2026.

For those unfamiliar, Damian Hamada returned to Earth after 35 years away to spearhead this apocalyptic metal endeavor. Working alongside vocalist Isha-do Sakura—a Okinawa native and X-Factor finalist—and the skilled musicians of progressive rock outfit Kanazawa Ebisu, D.H.C. has already built a formidable reputation in the global metal underground. Their previous albums consistently topped domestic metal streaming charts, and their conceptual approach to metal theater rivals anything in the modern Japanese metal scene.

![Damian Hamada's Creatures "Seven Deadly Sins" cover art](https://images.weserv.nl/?url=http%3A%2F%2Fvijuttoke.com%2Fcontents%2Fwp-content%2Fuploads%2F%25E3%2580%258C%25E4%25B8%2583%25E3%2581%25A4%25E3%2581%25AE%25E5%25A4%25A7%25E7%25BD%25AA%25E3%2580%258Dsmall-580x580.jpg&w=800&output=jpg)

In a characteristically philosophical statement, Damian Hamada expounds on the single's thematic core: that the seven deadly sins—pride, envy, wrath, greed, sloth, gluttony, and lust—aren't merely vices but the emotional and desire-driven forces that have propelled human civilization forward. Without them, countless historical revolutions, technological advances, and economic expansions would never have occurred. The overlord even poses a provocative question: if human sin and desire transcend divine and demonic control alike, aren't they ultimately the most powerful force in existence? His final twist? Perhaps the true architect of sin is God himself.

Beyond this single, expect major live activity. D.H.C. performs "The Worst 10" at Tokyo's Veats Shibuya on August 15, featuring a setlist voted on by official fanclub members. A nationwide tour dubbed "Tour 666" launches in November across seven Japanese cities, with stops in Saitama, Fukuoka, Okayama, Aichi, Osaka, and Tokyo.

This is essential material for metalheads seeking intelligent, theatrical storytelling woven into genuinely heavy compositions. Head to the band's official website for pre-order details, streaming information, and complete tour ticketing across all venue partners. Also explore their social channels—X, Instagram, Facebook, TikTok, and YouTube—for ongoing updates on their earthly manifestation.
