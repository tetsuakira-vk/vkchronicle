<p>Re:name have marked a major milestone by releasing their new album *1626* on their 10th anniversary, March 25th—and they're already announcing an expanded nationwide tour to match the momentum.</p>
<p>The album's title encapsulates the band's journey: formed when the members were just 16 years old, they've now spent a full decade together with the same lineup—a remarkable feat in today's music landscape. *1626* arrives as both a retrospective and a statement of intent, featuring eight tracks that showcase the band's evolution while maintaining the melodic rock foundation that's defined their decade-long run.</p>
<p>Leading the charge is "one room," which arrives with a music video that brings drummer Yamaken's songwriting vision to life in unexpected ways. Yamaken penned both the song's lyrics *and* a short story of the same name that inspired the video. He also wrote the script, while director Miwakana took the helm behind the camera. First pressings of *1626* include a physical booklet containing Yamaken's short story—a creative cross-media approach that gives fans something tangible beyond the usual album experience.</p>
<p>The digital tracklist runs to 8 songs (with two bonus cuts exclusive to the CD edition: "KISS ME HONEY" and "I've"), giving listeners a tightly curated snapshot of where Re:name stands in 2026.</p>
<p>On the live front, their 10th-anniversary free show at Osaka Castle Concert Hall drew 2,000 fans despite rainy weather, marking the largest single headline the band has ever pulled off. But they're not stopping there. Originally slated as an Osaka–Tokyo–Nagoya three-venue tour (CLUB QUATTRO series), Re:name have now added Fukuoka and Sendai dates, transforming it into their biggest tour yet.</p>
<p>**Tour dates:**</p>
<p>- **Osaka (Umeda CLUB QUATTRO):** May 23, 2026</p>
<p>- **Tokyo (Shibuya CLUB QUATTRO):** May 30, 2026  </p>
<p>- **Nagoya (CLUB QUATTRO):** June 14, 2026</p>
<p>- **Fukuoka & Sendai:** dates TBA</p>
<p>Tickets are ¥4,400 (plus drink charge). Grab *1626* now and secure your spot—this is peak Re:name.</p>
