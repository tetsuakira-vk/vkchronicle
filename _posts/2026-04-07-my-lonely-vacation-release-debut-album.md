<p>My Lonely Vacation is ready to unveil their first full-length album, and it's shaping up to be a compelling addition to the Japanese rock landscape. The 10-track record *BEAUTIFY* drops physically on April 29, 2026, with a digital release arriving three weeks earlier on April 7.</p>
<p>The band brings together an impressive lineup with deep roots in the Japanese rock underground. Vocalist Takahiro and drummer Rino both come from the disbanded group JILL-PRINCE, while guitarist Lise also spent time in JILL-PRINCE before collaborating with the unit Chicktak. Rounding out the quartet is guitarist Naki, completing a four-piece configuration built on chemistry and shared musical vision.</p>
<p>The album's tracklist reveals an ambitious sonic palette: opening with "Specialess" and flowing through standout cuts like "Toxic" (the lead single with an accompanying music video), "Labyrinth," "Middle Mermaid," "Valentine Kill," and the closing track "Lazurite." The production spans 10 songs across 36 minutes—a lean, focused statement that suggests the band isn't interested in filler.</p>
<p>If you haven't caught My Lonely Vacation yet, the full album preview is available on YouTube, giving fans a comprehensive listen before the official release. The "Toxic" music video is equally worth your time, showcasing the visual presentation backing this musical project.</p>
<p>For those eager to dive in immediately, *BEAUTIFY* is available through TuneCore Japan, meaning you can secure a digital copy from most major streaming and digital retail platforms come April 7. Physical copies will follow later that month.</p>
<p>Whether you're already familiar with the members' work in JILL-PRINCE or discovering them fresh, *BEAUTIFY* represents an exciting moment for a band clearly ready to make their mark with a proper album statement. Mark your calendars and check out the preview—this one's worth your attention.</p>
