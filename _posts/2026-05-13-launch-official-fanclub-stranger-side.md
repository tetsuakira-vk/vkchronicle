グラビティ (Gravity) have officially launched their first-ever fanclub, and Western supporters of the band now have a dedicated membership avenue to deepen their connection with the Visual Kei act.

Announced on May 13, 2026, the **グラビティ OFFICIAL FANCLUB 'STRANGER SIDE'** is now open for memberships at an annual fee of ¥6,000 (approximately $40–45 USD). The fanclub platform will operate through Club-Zy, a Japanese fanclub service provider widely used across the VK scene.

For those unfamiliar with グラビティ, the band has carved out a distinctive space in contemporary Visual Kei with their atmospheric soundscapes and theatrical live presence. The launch of a dedicated fanclub signals both their growing fanbase and a desire to build closer relationships with supporters.

**What Members Get**

The 'STRANGER SIDE' fanclub promises an array of member-exclusive perks: priority ticket sales for upcoming shows, exclusive blog content, fanclub-only streaming releases, and curated photo and video material. These benefits are standard across Japanese fanclubs but remain valuable for fans seeking earlier access and rare content.

**Inaugural FC-Only Concert**

To celebrate the launch, グラビティ will hold their first fanclub-exclusive live performance on **Wednesday, June 30, 2026** at **Otsuka HEARTS NEXT** in Tokyo. The show, also titled 'STRANGER SIDE,' provides early members an intimate concert experience before wider tour announcements potentially surface.

**How to Join**

International fans can typically access Club-Zy memberships through VPN services or by contacting Japanese proxy services if direct registration proves difficult from outside Japan. The ¥6,000 annual commitment breaks down to approximately ¥500 monthly—a reasonable investment for exclusive content and ticket priority during Japan's busy concert season.

For more details about membership benefits and to register, head directly to Club-Zy's official platform. Existing グラビティ fans should act quickly, as early membership often yields special bonuses or exclusive merch during fanclub launch windows.
