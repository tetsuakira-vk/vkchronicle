The hype around Fatüm's second Demonstration Showcase is already reaching fever pitch—VIP tickets sold out the moment they went on sale. If you're not familiar with the project yet, Fatüm is a magia gothic unit featuring SUI (formerly of DAVID) and HIDE-ZOU (D) on guitar. Their atmospheric, darkly elegant sound has been turning heads in the Visual Kei underground since their debut.

The sold-out status speaks volumes about fan anticipation for "Vox Dei II," scheduled for September 13 at EDGE Ikebukuro. Good news, though: a limited batch of additional VIP tickets will go on sale July 5 at 7:00 PM, so there's still a shot at those premium packages if you move fast.

Standard advance tickets remain available at 5,500 yen (plus venue fee), and day-of tickets will be 6,000 yen. The VIP experience includes early entry, an exclusive acrylic keychain, and a special CD titled "Eternal Night's Phantom Flower -Omen-," which differs from the venue-exclusive release dropping that same evening.

This is worth your attention beyond just the main event. Fatüm released their 1st Single "Canary" on June 24, and a second audio project will be exclusive to the September 13 show—a smart move that rewards those who make the trip to Tokyo. The bill also features support from bassist MASASHI (Versailles) and drummer 響 (Skyscraper Opera), adding serious musicianship to the lineup.

Doors open at 5:00 PM, with the show starting at 5:30 PM and wrapping around 7:00 PM—a tight, focused set that promises intensity.

Head to Vox Store (voxstore.stores.jp) starting July 5 at midnight JST to grab your tickets. For deeper context on Fatüm's sound and ongoing activities, visit their official website at fatum.jp, or follow them on X and Instagram for real-time updates.
