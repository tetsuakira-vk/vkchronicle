<h2>April 2026 Month in Review: Reunions, Supergroups & the Sound of Tomorrow</h2>

<p>April was the month Visual Kei stopped playing it safe—legends returned, new blood arrived, and the underground roared louder than ever.</p>
<!--more-->

<h3>Legends Return, New Era Begins</h3>

<p>The month's heartbeat was unmistakably nostalgic yet forward-looking. <a href="https://vknyusu.blogspot.com/">DIR EN GREY</a> unleashed <em>MORTAL DOWNER</em>, their most ambitious statement yet, with <a href="https://vknyusu.blogspot.com/">two separate reviews</a> cementing it as a masterwork of controlled chaos. The <a href="https://vknyusu.blogspot.com/">Tower Records Cafe collaboration</a> and <a href="https://vknyusu.blogspot.com/">Nico Nico livestream special</a> showed how the veterans continue to dominate the cultural conversation. Meanwhile, <a href="https://vknyusu.blogspot.com/">AREGE returned after 26 years</a> with a stunning new music video for "Monochrome"—proof that some bands' mystique only deepens with time. And in perhaps April's most electrifying moment, <a href="https://vknyusu.blogspot.com/">SCISSOR made a surprise reunion announcement</a> after two decades away, with streaming debuts and May live dates already locked in.</p>

<h3>The Supergroup Explosion</h3>

<p>April was the month of bold new collectives. <a href="https://vknyusu.blogspot.com/">YUKI from ex-Raphael formed "Solid Statement Soul"</a>, while <a href="https://vknyusu.blogspot.com/">Fatüm launched a special unit pairing SUI and HIDE-ZOU</a>. <a href="https://vknyusu.blogspot.com/">AIKOKU BARRACUDA emerged as a fresh supergroup</a> with a June debut live already scheduled, and <a href="https://vknyusu.blogspot.com/">Erzsébet Project formed</a> bringing gothic elegance with Monet and KD. <a href="https://vknyusu.blogspot.com/">KYMM united vistlip's Yuh with an ex-MELVEL vocalist</a>, while <a href="https://vknyusu.blogspot.com/">OLD CIRCUS assembled seasoned underground veterans</a> for an August EP drop. These weren't vanity projects—they were carefully curated power moves that signaled serious creative ambition.</p>

<h3>New Releases & Fresh Energy</h3>

<p><a href="https://vknyusu.blogspot.com/">fulusu delivered "Birth in Vitro"</a>, <a href="https://vknyusu.blogspot.com/">The Ravens released "BAND-AID"</a> with exclusive tour merch, and <a href="https://vknyusu.blogspot.com/">LAST ALLIANCE unveiled their 8th album "divine"</a>. <a href="https://vknyusu.blogspot.com/">KIRITO launched "THE SHIFT COMPLETE" tour</a> with a bold new sound direction, while <a href="https://vknyusu.blogspot.com/">lynch. announced "CLIMAX" and a national tour</a> following their celebrated 20th anniversary. Emerging acts stole hearts too: <a href="https://vknyusu.blogspot.com/">unRealless teased their debut EP "NETWORK SYNDROME"</a> arriving in May, <a href="https://vknyusu.blogspot.com/">PARAMENA dropped their djent-inflected debut digital EP "APEIRON EP"</a>, and <a href="https://vknyusu.blogspot.com/">THE MAYDAY MAYDAY prepared a free live-limited CD</a> that captured the underground's scrappy spirit.</p>

<h3>Tours & International Expansion</h3>

<p><a href="https://vknyusu.blogspot.com/">MUCC expanded their European footprint with Berlin & Helsinki dates</a>, while <a href="https://vknyusu.blogspot.com/">KAMIJO announced eight European cities for September</a>. <a href="https://vknyusu.blogspot.com/">Kaya & Schwarz Stein made their triumphant return to European stages after 14 years</a>, and <a href="https://vknyusu.blogspot.com/">SUI launched a Latin America acoustic solo tour</a>. The international Visual Kei conversation has never been louder.</p>
