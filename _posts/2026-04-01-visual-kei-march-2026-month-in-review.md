<h2>VKNews March 2026 Month in Review</h2>

<p><strong>March proved to be Visual Kei's most transformative month in years—a perfect storm of legacy moves, fresh blood, and the scene's most dramatic personnel shifts in recent memory.</strong></p>
<!--more-->

<h3>The Scene's Biggest Shakeup</h3>

<p>The month opened with seismic news that rippled across the entire fanbase: <a href="https://vknyusu.blogspot.com/">the GazettE, one of Visual Kei's most influential acts since their 2002 debut, parted ways with guitarist Aoi</a> amid internal conflict. It was the kind of lineup announcement that doesn't come around often, and it set the tone for a month defined by transformation and renewal. Later in March, LUNA SEA honored their irreplaceable late drummer Shnya with the announcement of <a href="https://vknyusu.blogspot.com/">a 33-show national tour launching May 29th</a>—a deeply moving tribute that captured the scene's collective spirit of resilience and remembrance.</p>

<h3>New Releases & Digital Momentum</h3>

<p>The release calendar exploded with activity. Digital singles dominated the landscape as <a href="https://vknyusu.blogspot.com/">HUMAN ERROR 2 dropped dual offerings "幸福人間" and "hate"</a>, while <a href="https://vknyusu.blogspot.com/">SCVMS made their official entrance with debut digital single "NEW ERA"</a>. <a href="https://vknyusu.blogspot.com/">VALHALLA launched an ambitious 12-month digital singles campaign</a>, signaling the scene's continued embrace of streaming-first strategies. Established acts weren't sitting idle either: <a href="https://vknyusu.blogspot.com/">Nobushige Takahashi announced his third solo single "Deus benedicat"</a>, while <a href="https://vknyusu.blogspot.com/">GOTCHAROCKA delivered a striking double A-side "0 MINUS 0 / Wicked Div.1"</a>. Looking ahead, <a href="https://vknyusu.blogspot.com/">DIR EN GREY revealed full artwork and tracklist details for their 12th studio album "MORTAL DOWNER"</a> (arriving April 8th), and <a href="https://vknyusu.blogspot.com/">vistlip announced "DAWN" drops April 1st alongside tour dates</a>.</p>

<h3>Tours, Revivals & Anniversary Celebrations</h3>

<p>March was defined by legends reclaiming the stage and fresh acts announcing their arrival. <a href="https://vknyusu.blogspot.com/">Eve announced their first live house tour in 7 years</a>, while <a href="https://vknyusu.blogspot.com/">KALEN surprised fans with a one-day 20th anniversary revival show</a>. The momentum extended to anniversary celebrations: <a href="https://vknyusu.blogspot.com/">YONA YONA WEEKENDERS marked their 10th anniversary with "Tokio Dungeon"</a>, <a href="https://vknyusu.blogspot.com/">Re:name celebrated a decade with album '1626' and an expanded tour</a>, and <a href="https://vknyusu.blogspot.com/">heidi. commemorated 20 years with best-album "MUSEUM"</a>. <a href="https://vknyusu.blogspot.com/">DEZERT completed their historic 47-prefecture tour and announced two new 2026 tours</a>—a jaw-dropping achievement that earned them instant legend status. The month also saw <a href="https://vknyusu.blogspot.com/">D'ERLANGER reveal "Evermore" album details and a massive 2026 tour schedule</a>, proving the legendary act remains unstoppable.</p>

<h3>Fresh Blood & New Projects</h3>

<p>The underground continued to flourish with exciting new formations. <a href="https://vknyusu.blogspot.com/">MeltyCΛlt launched as a new VK project from ex-members</a>, <a href="https://vknyusu.blogspot.com/">EPHEM announced their first live after official formation</a>, <a href="https://vknyusu.blogspot.com/">Sick. returned after a 7-year hiatus with a new lineup</a>, and <a href="https://vknyusu.blogspot.com/">CyberDope formed by uniting scene veterans</a>. Supergroups weren't far behind: <a href="https://vknyusu.blogspot.com/">ANCIENT MYTH unveiled their full lineup with Ba.Raimu on bass</a>, while <a href="https://vknyusu.blogspot.com/">ELISE emerged as a fresh supergroup with "SATELLITE" MV</a>. <a href="https://vknyusu.blogspot.com/">MASKED 2026 unveiled 57 Visual Kei vocalists in an epic photoshoot</a>, cementing itself as the scene's most ambitious collaborative festival.</p>
