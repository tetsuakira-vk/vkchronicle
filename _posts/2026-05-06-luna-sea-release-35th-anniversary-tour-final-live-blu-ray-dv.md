Legendary Visual Kei pioneers LUNA SEA are marking over three and a half decades of gothic majesty with the release of their 35th Anniversary Tour finale—captured live and now available on Blu-ray and DVD.

On February 23rd, 2026, LUNA SEA will unleash *35th ANNIVERSARY TOUR ERA TO ERA -THE FINAL EPISODE- LUNATIC TOKYO 2025 -Special GIG-*, documenting their monumental show from last year. For those unfamiliar with the band, LUNA SEA emerged from the early 90s Visual Kei scene as architects of ethereal, atmospheric rock—with frontman Ryuichi's haunting vocals, Sugizo's soaring guitar work, and the rhythm section's immaculate precision defining an entire era of Japanese rock.

The band has spent the past year documenting their anniversary tour across Japan, and this final live package captures the essence of their evolution across nearly four decades. The release comes in multiple formats: standard Blu-ray and DVD editions, plus limited versions featuring exclusive bonus items. Both video formats drop on February 25th, 2026, with pre-orders already live through major Japanese retailers including Amazon.co.jp, Tower Records Online, and Yahoo! Shopping.

The tracklist and exact runtime haven't been fully detailed in early announcements, but given the scale of their recent tour productions—which spanned multiple nights in Tokyo and beyond—fans can expect a comprehensive setlist spanning their most iconic tracks alongside deeper cuts that showcase LUNA SEA's unmatched catalogue.

LUNA SEA's decision to document this milestone through a live release rather than a studio album speaks volumes about where the band stands: their power remains undimmed on stage, their chemistry unshakeable, and their fanbase as devoted as ever. Three and a half decades into their career, they're proving that true artistry transcends trends.

If you've followed LUNA SEA's journey from the Visual Kei underground to arena-filling legends, this live release is essential. Even newer fans discovering their work will find this a perfect entry point into one of Japan's most influential rock bands. Pre-order your copy now through the links above.
