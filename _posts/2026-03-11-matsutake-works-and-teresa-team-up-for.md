<p>Two distinctive Visual Kei acts are joining forces for an upcoming two-man live that promises to be a must-see event for fans of Japan's underground rock scene.</p>
<p>**MATSUTAKE WORKS** and **TERESA** have announced *Hen na Ningen* (Strange Human), a joint live performance scheduled for **April 5th**. The title translates to "Strange Human"—a fitting name for what should be an intriguing collision of two unique musical aesthetics.</p>
<p>While both bands operate within the Visual Kei sphere, they bring distinctly different energies to the stage. **MATSUTAKE WORKS** has built a reputation for their dynamic, genre-blending approach that incorporates elements of rock, electronic, and theatrical presentation. Meanwhile, **TERESA** brings their own compelling flavor to the visual rock landscape, with a sound that balances aggressive musicianship with atmospheric depth.</p>
<p>For Western fans who may be less familiar with these acts, this two-man format is a fantastic opportunity to discover emerging talent in Japan's Visual Kei community. Two-man lives (or "two-mans") are a cornerstone of the Japanese underground rock scene, allowing bands to build deeper connections with audiences and showcase their material in an intimate yet high-energy setting.</p>
<p>**Event Details:**</p>
<p>- **Date:** April 5th</p>
<p>- **Event Name:** *Hen na Ningen*</p>
<p>- **Advance Ticket Price:** ¥4,500</p>
<p>- **Ticket On-Sale:** From the evening of March 5th</p>
<p>If you're intrigued by what MATSUTAKE WORKS and TERESA have to offer, advance tickets are already available. For those tracking the current Japanese Visual Kei landscape, this is a great chance to support both bands and experience what the underground scene has brewing. Keep an eye on their social media channels for venue details and additional information as the date approaches.</p>
