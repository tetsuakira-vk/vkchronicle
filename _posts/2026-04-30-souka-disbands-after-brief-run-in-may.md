<p>Another short-lived chapter closes in the Visual Kei underground: **Souka** (葬花, literally "Flower Burial") has announced their disbandment, set for May 2025.</p>
<p>The band, which only formed in December 2024, shared the news via Twitter today alongside a heartfelt message thanking fans for the memories they've created together during their brief existence. While specifics about the reasons for their split remain undisclosed, the group has promised to reveal their final live plans in the coming weeks—so fans wanting to catch them one last time should stay tuned to their official channels.</p>
<p>It's a bittersweet reminder of how transient the VK scene can be. New bands emerge with ambitious visions and fresh energy, only to dissolve before they've had time to build a substantial discography or touring momentum. Souka's lifespan of roughly five months is certainly on the shorter end, but it's far from unprecedented in Japan's underground rock sphere, where lineup instability, creative differences, or simply the grueling demands of the scene can derail even promising projects.</p>
<p>For Western fans who were just discovering Souka, this is disappointing timing—many international VK enthusiasts never get the chance to see emerging acts before they vanish. That said, their final lives will be an opportunity to witness their last performances and support them as they close this chapter.</p>
<p>If you were following Souka or are curious about their sound, make sure to check out their social media for announcements about their farewell shows. And if you're part of the broader VK community, use this as a reminder to support the bands you love while they're still around. The underground moves fast, and opportunities don't last forever.</p>
