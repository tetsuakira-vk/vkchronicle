<p>Just when you thought Kizu's new album was finally on the horizon, the Japanese Visual Kei band has pushed back their highly anticipated release once again—but not without giving fans something to sink their teeth into.</p>
<p>On March 26, Kizu announced that their upcoming album *Gokuraku Yori Gokujo no Ame* (極楽より極上の雨, literally "Rain More Sublime Than Paradise") will now arrive on August 5, 2026. This marks the second delay for the project; the band previously postponed it from an unannounced date to April 8 back in October 2025. Album delays are never fun, but Kizu is softening the blow with the digital release of two new singles: "JP:PARASITE" and "Houou."</p>
<p>For those unfamiliar with Kizu, the Tokyo-based outfit has carved out a distinctive niche within the Visual Kei scene with their blend of aggressive guitar work, theatrical aesthetics, and introspective lyricism. The band has built a loyal international following, particularly among Western VK fans who appreciate their uncompromising approach to both sound and image.</p>
<p>The decision to drop two singles ahead of the album's August release is a smart move—it keeps momentum alive and gives the fanbase tangible new material to explore while they wait for the full project. Both "JP:PARASITE" and "Houou" are now available on digital platforms, so you can start digging into what Kizu has been working on during this extended production period.</p>
<p>While album delays are frustrating, they often signal that a band is taking the time to refine their vision and deliver something truly worthwhile. Given Kizu's track record, there's every reason to believe *Gokuraku Yori Gokujo no Ame* will be worth the wait.</p>
<p>Head over to your favorite digital music platform now and check out "JP:PARASITE" and "Houou." Mark August 5 on your calendar, and stay tuned for more details on the full album as we get closer to release day.</p>
