<h2>VK Chronicle Month in Review: June 2026</h2>

<p><strong>June proved to be Visual Kei's most electrifying month in years—a perfect storm of legacy revivals, international expansion, festival dominance, and bittersweet farewells that left the scene buzzing with possibility and nostalgia in equal measure.</strong></p>
<!--more-->

<h3>Tours & Live Events</h3>

<p>The month's live landscape was dominated by massive announcements and record-breaking festivals. <a href="https://vkchronicle.com/2026/06/buglug-s-bagsami-2026-festival-breaks-vk-records-with-94-ban/">BugLug's Bagsami 2026 Festival shattered Visual Kei records with an astounding 94 bands</a>, cementing the event as the scene's most ambitious undertaking. Meanwhile, <a href="https://vkchronicle.com/2026/06/muque-release-happy-groovy-mv-2026-tour-announced/">muque announced their 2026 tour</a> with a vibrant new MV, and <a href="https://vkchronicle.com/2026/06/umbrella-announce-ambitious-tour-historic-osaka-bigcat-show/">umbrella revealed an ambitious nationwide tour capped by a historic Osaka BIG CAT show</a>. On the international front, <a href="https://vkchronicle.com/2026/06/b7klan-j-rock-fest-brings-japan-s-vk-elite-to-paris-this-jul/">the B7KLAN J-ROCK FEST brings Japan's VK elite to Paris this July</a>, featuring DIR EN GREY, the GazettE, and Versailles. Domestically, <a href="https://vkchronicle.com/2026/06/hero-babykingdom-lead-rengeki-event-lineups/">HERO and BabyKingdom led the "Rengeki" event lineups</a>, while emerging festival culture flourished with <a href="https://vkchronicle.com/2026/06/rock-heat-wave-2026-new-nagoya-festival-unites-vk-scene/">Rock Heat Wave 2026, a new Nagoya festival uniting the VK scene</a>.</p>
