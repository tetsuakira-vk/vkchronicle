There's a new name turning heads in the Visual Kei underground, and they're not holding back. Kuromitsu have just unveiled their debut music video for "君のハーフツインを食べたい" (I Want to Eat Your Half-Twin), a provocative title that hints at the band's unapologetic aesthetic.

For those unfamiliar, Kuroimitsu is a fresh outfit operating under the concept of "pure love" — but make no mistake, this isn't your typical romance narrative. The aggressive title belies something far more intricate: a meditation on relationships that exist outside conventional boundaries, filtered through Kuroimitsu's own distinctive visual and sonic philosophy.

The MV itself is a visual feast. Layered with black and pink tones, the imagery channels decadence and decay in equal measure. Butterflies serve as the central symbolic motif, anchoring a world where cuteness collides with madness, where raw physicality meets ephemeral beauty. It's the kind of deliberate contradiction that defines modern Visual Kei at its best.

The band is moving fast. They're set to play their debut live at the MASKED 2026 circuit event on May 23rd at Shibuya, Tokyo (DAY2), followed by an ambitious nationwide tour. Starting June 9th at Shibuya-Rei, Kuroimitsu will embark on a 47-prefecture one-man tour titled "純愛" (Pure Love), with the final show doubling as a birthday celebration on December 19th at Shibuya-Rei.

What's impressive here isn't just ambition — it's the coherence of vision. From the MV's aesthetic down to the tour conceptualization, everything feels intentional and thematically unified. This is a band that understands the Visual Kei format and isn't afraid to push its boundaries.

If you're curious about what Kuroimitsu are building, check out the debut MV and keep an eye on their tour schedule. This is one band worth watching closely.
