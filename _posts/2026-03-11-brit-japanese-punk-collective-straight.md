<p>It's the kind of opportunity most emerging bands can only dream about: as 2025 winds down, the Brit-Japanese punk outfit STRAIGHT BACK FORWARD will share the stage with some of Japan's most respected underground musicians at one of Tokyo's most prestigious venues.</p>
<p>On **30 December 2025**, the band will perform at **Shibuya O-EAST** as part of **Hokama Daibounenkai** (The Arsonist's Year-End Riot)—a star-studded year-end celebration curated by none other than **J**, the legendary LUNA SEA bassist and co-founder. It's a testament to STRAIGHT BACK FORWARD's rapid rise within Japan's punk underground.</p>
<p>The lineup reads like an underground scene all-star roster. Joining STRAIGHT BACK FORWARD are punk heavyweights **ROTTENGRAFFTY** and **BONE DAWN**, plus special guests **Toshiya** of the iconic DIR EN GREY and **Takada Metal** of ELLEGARDEN and MAYKIDZ. More performers are expected to be announced soon.</p>
<p>For those unfamiliar with STRAIGHT BACK FORWARD, the project centers on guitarist **masasucks**—a prolific sessioneer with credits spanning Fullscratch, the HIATUS, Radiots, and numerous other acts—alongside vocalist **Joshua** (NEATZ). The band deliberately rotates in guest musicians from Japan's thriving punk community, creating an ever-evolving sonic landscape that keeps things fresh and collaborative.</p>
<p>2025 has been a breakthrough year for the outfit. Their debut single, **'Don't Back Down / Haunted Home'** (released through London-based JPU Records), made serious waves, with the title track landing on over **200 Spotify playlists**. The band subsequently dropped a live performance video filmed during an October support slot for Dizzy Sunfist and GOOD4NOTHING at Shinjuku ACB, capturing their raw energy in real time.</p>
<p>If you haven't yet checked out 'Don't Back Down,' now's the perfect moment—it's a punchy, hook-laden anthem that showcases why J and the underground scene are paying attention. And if you're anywhere near Tokyo on New Year's Eve eve, a ticket to Hokama Daibounenkai should absolutely be on your radar.</p>
