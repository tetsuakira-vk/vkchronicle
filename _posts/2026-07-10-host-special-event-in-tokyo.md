Fresh off their ambitious 47-prefecture nationwide tour, emerging Visual Kei act 黒蜜 (Kurogomitsu) are doubling down on momentum with a curated showcase event this September.

On September 6th, the band will host "黒蜜の日" (Kurogomitsu Day) at Tokyo's Shibuya-ku venue BlackHole in Ikebukuro. The lineup features 黒蜜 alongside fellow acts 東京花嫁 (Tokyo Hanayome), BLAZE, and 曇りのち (Kumori no Chi)—a deliberately diverse bill designed to create what organizers are calling "a special night where different worlds collide."

For those unfamiliar with 黒蜫, the band launched mere months ago and immediately committed to an audacious "pure love" (純愛) one-man tour spanning all 47 Japanese prefectures. It's the kind of grassroots ambition that defines underground VK culture. What's set them apart beyond raw determination is their innovative approach to fan engagement: they've implemented a "value box" system where attendees literally decide what their ticket is worth, plus location-specific surprise elements at each stop. The band is also gaining traction on social media—their track "君のハーフツインを食べたい" (I Want to Eat Your Half-Twins) has surpassed 170,000 plays on TikTok alone.

The September 6th event sits strategically in the middle of their nationwide gauntlet, designed as a spiritual centerpiece that distills the tour's conceptual world into one concentrated performance. Think of it as less promotional pit stop and more artistic statement—a chance to recalibrate before pushing toward the tour's finale.

Speaking of which: the 47-prefecture tour concludes December 19th at Tokyo's SHIBUYA REX, giving fans a final major milestone to anticipate.

For ticket details and further information, check 黒蜜's official channels and the venue's website directly. This is exactly the kind of DIY-spirited event worth supporting if you're invested in where underground VK is heading.
